District of Saanich Timeline

1906 District of Saanich incorporated; Thomas A. Brydon is the first Reeve. Saanich Police Department is established with 1 officer.
1911

1915

 Construction of new Municipal Hall at Royal Oak. Population reaches 6,026.

Saanich Water Works is established. Population is 10,056
1917 Saanich Fire Department is established and the No. 1 Fire Hall opens on Douglas Street.
1951

1956

 Ward Six secedes from the Municipality Of Saanich and is incorporated as Central Saanich.

Saanich commemorates its Golden Jubilee, and Grace Shaw becomes the first woman to serve on Saanich Council. Population is 38,358.
1958 Saanich Parks Department is established.
1959

1962

 Saanich Planning Department is formed. Municipal Yard moves to McKenzie Avenue.

The new Police and Fire Hall opens on Vernon Avenue.
1965

1966

 Opening of the new Saanich Municipal Hall on Vernon Avenue.

Saanich population reaches 59,000, surpassing Victoria.
1968 George Pearkes Arena opens.
1971 Gordon Head Recreation Centre opens.
1973 Cedar Hill Community Centre opens.
1981

1993

 The new Coat of Arms is unveiled for the 75th anniversary of the Municipality.

Saanich Commonwealth Place recreation centre opens in time for the Commonwealth Games of 1994.
2006 Saanich celebrates its centennial.
