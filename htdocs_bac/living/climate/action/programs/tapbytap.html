The District of Saanich is a proud partner in Tap by Tap 2013!

If you are a building manager or property owner, you can save money and take action on climate change with this free program.  Tap by Tap is available on a first come, first serve basis for up to 3,000 residential units in the region. As an added bonus, each participating building will receive a customized walk-through assessment of further ways to save money through retrofits in common areas like hallways and laundry rooms. Why did more than 1,000 residents participate in Tap By Tap?

By installing an Energy and Water Saving Kit,they:

  • Saved over 40,000 litres of water each year
  • Saved $70 to $135 on your energy bills each year
  • Reduced greenhouse gas emissions generated through water heating
  • Enhanced drinking water quality by maintaining higher water levels in Sooke Lake
  • Defered the need for developing new sources of drinking water
  • Reduced the costs, energy and materials required to treat and deliver water

If only 600 households in Saanich switched to low-flow fixtures, would annually save:

  • 25,185,000 litres of water (that’s about 10 Olympic Swimming Pools)
  • 935,000 KWh of energy
  • 89 tonnes of greenhouse gases

Did you know?

  • The average British Columbian uses 490 litres of water per day
  • This is more than 48% more water than Canadian average
  • Low-flow fixtures can reduce water consumption by 20% to 50%
  • One third of all energy use in a typical home goes to heating water
  • For more water saving tips, visit the CRD Water Saving Tips page or the Natural Resources Canada Better Water Use page.
  • For more information about our source of public water, visit the CRD Water Storage page
