Are you a Saanich Carbon Champion? Join the many residents who are already taking action on climate change while helping Saanich meet our community greenhouse gas (GHG) reduction target of 33% below 2007 levels by 2020. Simply follow our 1-2-3 step process that will guide you to reducing your carbon footprint while saving you big money at the pump and on increasing energy bills.

Join us for this engaging evening with those who are leading change in Saanich and throughout the Province of BC. This informative night will provide inspiration through the people and projects that are reducing emissions and costs in Saanich.

As a community, Saanich has made significant strides in our efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The municipality has worked hard to support carbon champions in the community who are setting the "new normal" for residents and businesses in Saanich. With presentations from:

Keynote Speaker Rob Abbott, Executive Director, BC Climate Action Secretariat

Rob joined CAS last year, after 20 years of providing sustainability guidance to many companies throughout Canada and internationally. In his new role as Executive Director of Carbon Neutral Government and Climate Action Outreach for the Climate Action Secretariat (CAS) he helps to drive change to achieve British Columbia’s greenhouse gas (GHG) emission reduction targets by coordinating climate action across Government and with stakeholders. The focus of his discussion will be highlighting the efforts within the Province and government sector and how they connect to residents in the community.

Event Host:

  • Mark Boysen, Sustainability Division Lead, Saanich Planning Department

Saanich Carbon Champions Panel:

  • Rob Bernhadt - Builder of the first solar passive home on Vancouver Island.
  • Karen Jawl - Sustainable business leader with Saanich business Jawl Properties Ltd.
  • Ed Knaggs and family - Saanich award winners for their sustainability efforts at home.

We will also profile municipal projects that highlight our philosophy of leading by example and then help to connect you with tools and resources you need to lead change in your own home and workplace.
