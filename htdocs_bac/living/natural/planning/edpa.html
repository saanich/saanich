The Environmental Development Permit Area (EDPA) was adopted by Saanich Council on March 12, 2012.

The objectives of this Development Permit Area are to:

  • Protect the areas of highest biodiversity within Saanich;
  • Require mitigation during development; and
  • Require restoration to damaged or degraded ecosystems during development.
For an overview of the EDPA,  please see our brochure to the left.

What areas are included in the EPDA?

The EDPA consists of Environmentally Significant Areas (ESA’s) and buffers where needed.  Five inventories were used to identify the ESA’s included in the EDPA. Many of these inventories are conveniently included on our public GIS mapping system. To make sure you are consulting the map that was adopted by Council, please see the official EDPA Atlas. The EDPA does not include streams and riparian areas which already appear in the Streamside Development Permit Area.

The EDPA is based on the following inventories:

  • Sensitive Ecosystem Inventory, 2002 (Ministry of Environment & Environment Canada).  This Federal/Provincial inventory identifies ecosystems that are considered sensitive including woodlands, coastal bluff, older forest, and others. http://www.env.gov.bc.ca/sei/
  • Red and blue listed animals, plants and ecological communities (Conservation Data Centre, Ministry of Environment); The CDC tracks rare ecosystems, plants and animals and publishes maps, status reports, and other useful information. http://www.env.gov.bc.ca/cdc/
  • Wildlife Trees identified by the Wildlife Tree Stewardship Program (WiTS) (Federation of BC Naturalists) such as Bald Eagle and Great Blue Heron nests. http://www.wildlifetree.ca/
  • Isolated wetlands and watercourses, 2010 (District of Saanich) provide important habitat but are not fish-bearing. 
  • Saanich Marine Inventory, 2000 (District of Saanich and the Veins of Life Watershed Society).

When is an Environmental Development Permit needed?

The alteration of land, subdivision and construction are prohibited within an environmental development permit area unless an exemption applies or a development permit is issued.  A permit is generally required for the following activities:

  • Removal, alteration, disruption or destruction of vegetation;
  • Removal, deposit or disturbance of soils;
  • Construction or erection of buildings and structures;
  • Creation of non-structural impervious or semi-impervious surfaces;
  • Construction of roads, trails, docks, wharves and bridges;
  • Provision and maintenance of sewer and water services; and
  • Subdivision of land where there is the potential to create conditions for impacts to an ESA.

There are also many exemptions.  Please contact Saanich Environmental Services to discuss your project and identify any exemptions.
