The District of Saanich will acquire 32 acres of land in the Blenkinsop Valley at $1,250,000 for park and trail connections.

Staff Report to Mayor and Council

Blenkinsop Lake - Land Acquisition Map

 

This was an opportunity to acquire important park and trail connections identified in the Blenkinsop Local Area Plan (LAP).  The land includes portions of Blenkinsop Lake and adjacent riparian and environmentally sensitive areas, property adjacent to Beckwith and Valewood Parks, and interconnecting areas to accommodate trails and provide additional buffer between urban neighbourhood and agriculture operations.  There will be space to increase riparian vegetation within the floodplain and trails can be located with minimal disturbance to vegetation and habitat.  There will also be opportunities to restore sections of Beckwith and Grosbeak Creeks.  The acquisition includes four acres of Garry Oak ecosystem, the rarest ecosystem in British Columbia.

Acquiring the water license will also potentially augment fish habitat and environmental flows into Blenkinsop Creek, which feeds into Swan Creek and eventually the Colquitz River. 

The acquisition creates priority trail opportunity connections to link Beckwith Park to the Lochside Regional Trail, Valewood Park to Beckwith Park, and loops within the neighbourhood.  The additional recreational opportunities will increase public access to the area and in turn have a positive impact on environmental stewardship and agricultural awareness within the community.

The acquisition is supported by the Blenkinsop LAP and the Official Community Plan.  Council will fund the acquisition from two reserve funds - Municipal Wide Parks and Sub Regional Municipal Parks.
