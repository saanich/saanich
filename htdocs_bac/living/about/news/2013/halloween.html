Special Permits are available to residents who wish to have fireworks or a Halloween Bonfire on Thursday, October 31

More Information

Fireworks Regulation Bylaw, No. 8865

Special Permits are available to residents who wish to have a Halloween Bonfire on Thursday, October 31 and subject to the following conditions:

  1. The fire is to be supervised by a competent person until extinguished.
  2. The fire is to be situated at least 7.6 metres (25 feet) from any building fence or property line.
  3. A garden hose, hooked up and charged, is to be readily available.
  4. Additional equipment (shovels, rakes, etc.) as may be deemed necessary for fire control by the Issuing Officer is to be readily available.
  5. The permit is valid from 5pm to 11pm on October 31 only..
  6. The fire is to be completely extinguished by 11pm.

Bonfire permits are available at the Saanich Fire Department or any Saanich Fire Hall. The deadline to apply for a bonfire permit is 12 noon on October 31.

To make arrangements for a Halloween Bonfire Permit or for further information, please contact our Fire Prevention Division at (250) 475-5500.

A MUNICIPAL TICKET FOR $100 WILL BE ISSUED FOR ANY FIRES WITHOUT PERMITS.

 

  • Everyone who possesses/discharges fireworks must have a valid Fireworks permit.
  • Permits are issued only to persons 18 years of age and older.
  • Consumer fireworks discharges may only take place on Thursday, October 31, between 5pm and 10pm.
  • No fireworks may be sold or traded in the Municipality of Saanich.

Residents are encouraged to review the Fireworks Regulation Bylaw, No. 8865, for full information before planning any fireworks event.

As a condition of the permit, a Fireworks Safety Course is MANDATORY for those wishing to use consumer fireworks. The Saanich Fire Department offers this course as a public safety initiative.  Fireworks Permit applicants must choose and attend one of the Fireworks Safety Course workshops offered below.

PRE-REGISTRATION FOR THE WORKSHOPS IS MANDATORY. Register by email and please include your name, telephone number and the number for the workshop you wish to attend or contact the Fire Prevention Division at 250-475-5500.
