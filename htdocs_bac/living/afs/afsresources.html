The following provides sources and ideas to encourage agriculture within Saanich’s urban communities; to connect people with tools and resources to assist them in growing food; and to increase the acceptance of agriculture within Saanich’s urban areas.

Community Groups & Organizations

LifeCycles

A non-profit organization “dedicated to cultivating awareness and initiating action around food, health and urban sustainability in Greater Victoria.”  Their web site promotes garden sharing, community gardens, and food growing education. 

  • Local Food Directory
  • LifeCycles Urban Agriculture Hub an interactive community-driven “agricultural hub” that connects Victoria residents to each other and to the resources, knowledge and tools necessary when growing food within the city.
  • Lifecycles organizes the Sharing Backyards program for the Victoria area that connects people wishing to grow a vegetable garden to property owners that wish to share their garden space with others .

CR-FAIR

Capital Region Food and Agriculture Roundtable

Mission: To increase knowledge of and bring about positive change in the food and agriculture system in the Capital Region.

  • Food Matters e-newsletter
  • Food Related Events Calendar
  • CR-FAIR 2009 Food Resource Directory

Saanich Organics

Saanich Organics is a community of farmers from small, certified organic farms who work together and support each other in many ways.  The farmers connected with Saanich Organics work to deliver fresh, healthy, local, certified organic produce to the Great Victoria area.  They sell at the Moss Street Market and the James Bay Market; they run a  residential box delivery program, and provide produce to local restaurants and grocery stores.  The group launched a seed company in June 2011 called "Seeds of Revolution".

Gorge Tillicum Urban Farmers

A group of people (neighbours) sharing information about growing food and talking about issues related to food production.  If you’d like to know more, or if you would like to come to one of GTUF’s monthly meetings, contact Gabe Epstein belle.leon1@gmail.com to be added to the mailing list, or check out the new web site.

Haliburton Farms

A community organic farm that operates on land owned by the District of Saanich.  The farm has a board of directors and 5 separate businesses operating under the Farm Society that leases the land from the municipality.  The Society’s mission is to:

  • practice and promote sustainable farming practices
  • involve the community in sustainable food production
  • support a local sustainable food system
  • demonstrate the viability of small-scale farming
  • restore and enhance biodiversity within farming systems
  • demonstrate a cooperative community farming system
  • educate about agroecology

The Horticultural Centre of the Pacific is 103-acres of demonstration gardens, conservation area, a college and bistro welcoming visitors.  The Centre’s mission is “to enrich our community by sharing the beauty and joy of gardening, and by demonstrating the importance of plant diversity to life in our world.”  Courses in growing & preserving produce are offered at the Centre.

Grow a Row Organization

Distributes extra produce from local gardens to local people who would like to eat it!

City Harvest

An urban farming co-operative that transforms backyards and unused urban spaces into thriving food gardens, providing an abundance of healthy, accessible food for our communities and a sustainable livelihood for our members.

Bee information

The Provincial Government Ministry of Agriculture provides information on Orchard bees.  These bees are particularly well adapted in the northern ranges of blooming fruit trees.  Bee populations are an important aspect of garden and food production.

Urban Farming Stories & Education

Farm Folk / City Folk

Farm Folk / City Folk is a non-profit organization that encourages community engagement in the celebration of food. The group started in the Vancouver area and now holds events on Vancouver Island.

City Farmer

A website run from the office of the Vancouver Compost Demonstration Garden.  This website “teaches people how to grow food in the city, compost their waste and take care of their home landscape in an environmentally responsible way.”  They provide a collection of stories about urban farmers from around the world.

Nut Production

The Capital Nut Group works to “support the planting, care, and maintenance of edible nut trees on public and private land throughout the region.” 

BC Food Security Gateway

A web initiative of the Ministry of Healthy Living & Sport in partnership with the BC Health Authorities.  The web site is designed to be a “one stop” web resource for food security practitioners and others in British Columbia who want to build food secure communities and to make “healthy eating the easy choice.”

The US National Sustainable Agriculture Information Service

Funded by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Business-Cooperative Service provides information and other technical assistance to farmers, ranchers,  educators, and others involved in sustainable agriculture in the United States, but the publications offer information that may be beneficial to any farmer as they generally discuss how to assess, improve and maintain food system components

Gaia College

Gaia holds courses on growing food at Royal Roads University.  The course includes sessions on Foundation of Health, Veggies, Fruit-nuts-berries, Water wise management, etc.

Courses offered on organic land care including growing food are also identified through Saanich’s Environmental Planning web pages.

Sustainable Living and Urban Gardening Skills

SLUGS runs an educational website on growing your own food  and they run workshops for youth ages 13 to 30 who want to learn more about growing their own food.

ALM Farm

An organic farm located in Sooke where a group of farmers practice living sustainably by growing, harvesting, preserving, and marketing food and teaching others how to do the same.
