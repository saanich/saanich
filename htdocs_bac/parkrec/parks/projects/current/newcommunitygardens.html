Saanich’s Community Garden Policyrecognizes the need for community gardens and outlines ways that the Municipality will support existing gardens and the establishment of future community gardens. In the Strategic Plan 2013-2017 Council affirmed theirdirection to have one garden per local area for a total of 12 by the year 2036. Saanich Parks is pleased to announce two new Community Garden sites are under consideration.

The Parks and Recreation Department have been workingwithtwo community groups toexplore the possibility of establishing two new community garden sites, one in Gorge Park and one behind Saanich Commonwealth Place.

Proposed Gorge Tillicum Allotment Garden Site Facts

The District of Saanich recognizes the need for local food security and the development of community gardens. Both the Official Community Plan and the Strategic Plan 2013-2017 support agricultural initiatives within the municipality. The open field area in Gorge Park is the proposed location for a community allotment garden in the Tillicum Local Area. A local Gorge Park Community Garden Working Group, supported by the Gorge Tillicum Community Association and Gorge Tillicum Urban Farmers, has been established to collaborate with Saanich in the establishment of a community garden.

Overview

Part of the open field located in Gorge Park has been proposed as the site for a potential community garden. Saanich will provide the land and the Gorge Tillicum Community Association will oversee operation and administration of the garden.

Fact Sheet

  1. Saanich supports community gardening and will provide the area shown on the map for a garden site.
  2. A user agreement between Saanich and the local community association will outline the roles and responsibilities for the operation of the garden.
  3. Access to the allotment space in the community garden will be open to any resident of the District of Saanich. Further details to be worked out during the planning process.
  4. The selection of the potential site was determined by several factors including:
    • availability of parking, existing 20 stalls for Gorge Park
    • provision of water, connection available to an existing municipal water main
    • accessibility to public transit, in close proximity to Gorge Road
    • commitment from a volunteer gardening group to manage and operate the site
  5. Saanich will assist in site development activities such as planning and design, surveying, clearing, and irrigation improvements.

Public Process

Members of the garden working group have undertaken a significant amount of work to inform the local community about the proposal and to work with them to understand and alleviate any concerns they may have. This work has been underway since late 2011.

Saanich Parks and Recreation hosted a public open house on April 16, 2013 at the Pearkes Recreation Centre. This provided an opportunity for all members of the public to learn more about the project. Members of GTUF, as well as Saanich staff, were present to answer questions, provide information and hear feedback from the community.

Open House - Feedback Summary

The survey for the proposed Community Allotment Garden at Gorge Park closed on May 3rd. Thank you for providing feedback and the results of the survey will help us inform a decision for the proposed Community Allotment Garden. The full survey results are available by clicking here.

Survey Results

Overall, Community Gardens in Saanich received 91% support.

  • There was a minimal percentage of respondents who are current members of a community allotment gardening group (9%).
  • The support for the proposed location for the community allotment garden is as follows; 78 % Yes, 16 % No, 6 % Unsure.
  • 78 % of respondents supported the opportunity to experience gardening at this location.
  • 16 % of respondents did not support this location for a variety of reasons.
  • 56 % of respondents were not interested in joining a community allotment garden group.
  • There were many responses to the types of barriers for joining a community garden, such as personal time, wait lists, and lack of opportunity.
  • Residents that lived Within a 5 minute walk of Gorge Park made up the single largest group of respondents at 50%.

Saanich Parks, Planning & Design is continuing to work with the community group on design details for the garden layout. Click here to view the preliminary garden concept design. The proposal has been endorsed by the three advisory committees including Parks, Trails & Recreation, Environment and Natural Areas, and Healthy Saanich Advisory Committees. The proposal for Gorge Park Community Allotment Garden will be presented to Council in October (date to be determined).

Saanich Commonwealth Place Allotment Garden Site Facts

Overview

The open field area located behind Saanich Commonwealth Place has been proposed as a site for a potential community garden. Saanich would provide the land to be used as an allotment garden and a non-profit organization would be sought to operate the community garden. Saanich Allotment Garden Advocates have been assisting Saanich staff up to this point.

A public open house has taken place to gauge local support and gather feedback from local residents.

Fact Sheet

  • Saanich supports community gardening and would provide the area shown on the map for a garden site should there be local support.
  • A user agreement between Saanich and a non-profit organization (potentially SAGA) would outline the roles and responsibilities for the operation of the garden.
  • Membership to the allotment garden would be open to any resident of the District of Saanich.
  • The selection of the potential site was determined by consideration of the following:
    • availability of parking, existing 356 stalls at Commonwealth Place
    • provision of water, connection available to an existing municipal water main
    • accessibility to public transit, in close proximity to the Royal Oak Exchange
    • commitment from a volunteer gardening group to manage and operate the site
  • Saanich would assist in site development activities such as planning and design, surveying, clearing, and irrigation improvements.

Public Process

Saanich Parks and Recreation hosted a public open house on February 4, 2013 at the Saanich Commonwealth Place providing an opportunity for all members of the public to learn more about the project. Members of SAGA, as well as Saanich staff, were present to answer questions, provide information and hear feedback from the community.

Open House - Feedback Summary

Attendance

The Open House was held on February 4, 2013 from 4:00 - 7:30pm. 85 people signed in and a number of others passed through. 27 surveys were completed at the Open House. Presentation boards were left for viewing at Saanich Commonwealth Place until February 15th. A total of 340 completed surveys were received.

The online survey was available from February 5 to February 22, 2013. Some key survey highlights are listed below with the full survey results available by clicking here.

Survey Results

Overall, Community Gardens in Saanich received 68% support.

  • There was a minimal percentage of respondents who are current members of a community allotment gardening group (5%).
  • The support for the proposed location for the community allotment garden is as follows; 36 % Yes, 59 % No, 5 % Unsure.
  • 36 % of respondents supported the opportunity to experience gardening at this location.
  • 59 % of respondents did not support this location for a variety of reasons.
  • 72 % of respondents were not interested in joining a community allotment garden group.
  • There were many responses to the types of barriers for joining a community garden, such as personal time, wait lists, and lack of opportunity.
  • Residents that lived Elsewhere in Saanich made up the single largest group of respondents at 40%.

Next Steps

Thank you for taking the time to provide us your input. This feedback will be used to help inform an impending decision about the garden proposal.

Given the low level of support at this time, the garden proposal is on hold. Keep watching this space for any future updates on the status of this proposal.
