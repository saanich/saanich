Site Description

This site is located on either side of Mayfair Drive at the first parking area as the road comes up from Cedar Hill X Road.  The restoration area is on the north-facing slope bounded by public trails.  The site has some original plant species composition intact, but has been impacted by invasive species, garden species and human impacts.

Area: GORP site is approx. 0.46 ha

Elevation: 91 – 99 m

Aspect: 340°

Moisture regime: subxeric

There are two distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Grassland over Shallow Soils

Open Woods over Moderate Soils

History

By 1891, Cedar Hill X Road and Mayfair Drive were in place and this site was being used as parkland.  Development began in 1958 and Mayfair Drive was re-routed, parking lots developed and paths cleared.  The reservoir was constructed in 1967.  Stewardship of the Garry oak ecosystems at Mt. Tolmie Park has been active since 1995.

Restoration

Originally the adjacent sides of Mayfair Drive were chosen to compare and monitor two very different conditions as the west side was dense Scotch broom in 2000.  The broom was removed anonymously before comparisons and monitoring could be done.  Restoration on this site has included removal of invasive species and some garden species that were planted in the 1960’s.  Some native planting has occurred and management of human impacts to the site.  In 2005 a fire went through the west side of the site, providing an interesting opportunity to monitor these impacts.

Some native flora species commonly found at the Mt. Tolmie GORP Site include:

Quercus garryana Garry oak
Arbutus menziesii Arbutus
Symphoricarpos albus snowberry
Philadelphus lewisii mock orange
Camassia quamash common camas
Camassia leichtlinii great camas
Brodiaea coronaria harvest brodiaea
Brodiaea hyacinthina fool’s onion
Lomatium utriculatum spring gold
Polypodium glycyrrhiza licorice fern

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At the Mt. Tolmie GORP site some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including: the Mt. Tolmie Conservancy Association and the Mt. Douglas Secondary Environment Club.

Mt. Tolmie Park is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
