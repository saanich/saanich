Site Description

Located on McKenzie Ave near the Braefoot overpass, this site is part of the larger Feltham Park.  This site is a shrub dominated woodland with a small rocky outcrop in the centre.  The GORP site includes areas of municipal right-of way to the south and east.  There is no official trail in the GORP site, but a pathway used by locals and volunteers begins at the GORP sign on the east side along a public right-of-way. 

Area: GORP site is approx. 0.3 ha

Elevation: 40 – 47 m

Aspect: 235°

Moisture regime: submesic to mesic

There are two distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Rocky Outcrops and Thin Soils

Mature Woods over Deep Soils

History

In the late 1800’s this site was open deciduous woods near farmland.  There were no roads in the area at the time.  In the late 1950’s the area was cleared and development began to occur.  McKenzie Ave was built in 1969.

Restoration

This restoration site has had significant challenges due to English ivy.  Many truck-loads of ivy have been pulled and cut from this site, releasing trees and the understory from this invasive blanket.  Restoration has included removal and control of other invasive species such as Himalayan blackberry and daphne .  A large part of the site is dominated by dense shrubs such as Indian plum.  Some experimental thinning and removal, along with planting is being undertaken in a few areas.  This site also has significant human impacts including dumping and invasive spread from other properties.

Some native flora species commonly found at the Feltham Park GORP Site include:

Quercus garryana Garry oak
Oemleria cerasiformis  Indian plum
Symphoricarpos albus snowberry
Rosa nutkana Nootka rose
Philadelphus lewisii mock orange
Camassia quamash common camas
Brodiaea coronaria harvest brodiaea
Polypodium glycyrrhiza licorice fern
Plantanthera unalascensis Alaska rein-orchid

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At Feltham Park some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including: Cedar Hill Middle School, Glenlyon Norfolk School and ESL organizations. 

Feltham Park is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
