Site Description

This site is located in the Cedar Hill area, bordered by McRae Ave, Cedar Hill and Wetherby Roads.  This is a small, neighbourhood park with a fringe of Garry oaks around the border and a small remnant of woodland across McRae Ave bordering private properties. 

Area: GORP site is approx. 0.34 ha

Elevation: 35 - 38 m

Aspect: 170°

Moisture regime: submesic to mesic

There is one distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Open Woods over Moderate Soils

History

This area was cleared for farmland in the late 1800’s.  By 1939 there was increasing rural development near this site and by 1956 it was surrounded by roads.

Restoration

Wetherby Park is challenged by having been filled in the past.  Restoration has included applying leaf mulch, planting (including native shrubs, bulbs, grasses and oaks) and invasive species removal.  The small woodland across McRae has a rocky outcrop and some original species intact.  This site is a good educational project.

Some native flora species commonly found at the Wetherby Park GORP Site include:

Quercus garryana Garry oak
Camassia quamash  common camas
Camassia leichtlinii great camas

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At the Wetherby Park GORP site some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including: Scouts Canada and volunteers from ESL organizations.

Wetherby Park is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
