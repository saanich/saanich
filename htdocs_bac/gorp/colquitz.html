Site Description

The Mahon Brook site is located at 4334 Viewmont Street near the Royal Oak Shopping Centre.  Adjoining Brydon Park, Mahon Brook is within the Colquitz Park and the Centennial Trail system.  This site was very disturbed and has only one remnant mature Garry oak.  Brydon Park borders the site to the west, Mahon Brook (Colquitz tributary) to the south and private homes to the north.  In 2005, the trail through the site was made part of the Centennial Trail system.

Area: GORP site is approx. 0.35 ha

Elevation: 21 – 27 m

Aspect: 176°

Moisture regime: mesic to poorly drained riparian

There is one distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Mixed Vegetation over Clayey Soils

History

The area of this site was homestead in 1853.  By 1908, rural density began to increase and farmland subdivided, which continued through to the 1950’s.  A 1956 map shows the site as being cleared.  In the 1980’s suburban development began. 

Restoration

Restoration at the Mahon Brook site has included soil amendments, planting of Garry oaks and native plants and management of invasive species.  Himalayan blackberry, Scotch broom, Canada thistle, orchard grass and other invasive plant species are being managed at this site. 

Some native flora species commonly found at the Mahon Brook GORP Site include:

Quercus garryana Garry oak
Oemleria cerasiformis Indian plum
Symphoricarpos albus snowberry
Rosa nutkana Nootka rose
Holodiscus discolor ocean spray
Rubus ursinus trailing blackberry
Camassia quamash common camas

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At Mahon Brook some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including: the Royal Oak Community Association and Boy Scouts of Canada.

Mahon Brook is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
