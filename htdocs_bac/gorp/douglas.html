Site Description

This site is located at the top of Mt. Douglas, southeast of the lookout at the summit.  The site is accessed via Churchill Road, which is closed daily from 11 pm to 12noon.  The restoration site is composed of a depression on the south-facing slope below the look out.

Area:

Elevation: 207 – 218 m

Aspect: 180°

Moisture regime: xeric

There are two distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Grassland over Shallow Soils

Mixed Vegetation and Soils

History

Mt. Douglas was logged in 1843 by the Hudson’s Bay Company to supply timber for Fort Victoria.  In 1858 the area became a government reserve.  The building of the road through the park began over an existing trail in 1880 and was completed in the early 1990’s.  On September 30, 1889, thanks to Sir James Douglas, the Province of British Columbia granted Mount Douglas to the Corporation of the City of Victoria “upon trust to maintain and preserve the same as a public park or pleasure ground for the use, recreation and enjoyment of the public”.

Restoration

Restoration at the Mt. Douglas Summit GORP site has focused primarily on invasive species removal and monitoring.  The most significant invasive plant species has been Scotch broom.  Early native plantings were unsuccessful.  A significant issue for restoration at this site is problem solving in regards to high populations of non-native rabbits.

Some native flora species commonly found at the Mt. Douglas Summit GORP Site include:

Quercus garryana Garry oak
Holodiscus discolor ocean spray
Symphoricarpos albus snowberry
Camassia quamash common camas
Sanicula crassicaulis Pacific sanicle

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At Mt Douglas Summit some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including the Friends of Mt. Doug.

Mt. Douglas Park is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
