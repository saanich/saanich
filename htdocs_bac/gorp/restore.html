Celebrating 10 Years of Stewardship

They say “time flies when you’re having fun” – and that is certainly the case for all of us who are amazed that we have reached GORP’s 10th anniversary.

Ten years ago this November, the first official meeting of partners began Saanich’s Garry Oak Restoration Project. Since then GORP has put a great deal of work into nine active restoration sites and has developed a dynamic outreach and stewardship program. After ten years, maybe it’s a good time to ponder what makes a project like this successful?

Restoring Eden
A walk through Playfair Park can be like taking a walk back in time...
When Europeans first came to Southern Vancouver Island, there was a great attraction to the open oak meadows that once spread throughout what is now the Greater Victoria area and beyond. Compared to the dense forest along much of this coastal region, the oak landscapes were described as “a perfect Eden”(James Douglas, HBC Chief Factor 1843).
The settlement and development that grew from that point has left little remaining of these ecosystems that sustained local First Nations and first attracted Europeans.
