The winning submission from the public art competition for the new Arts Centre at Cedar Hill is a proposal by glass artist Rick Silas. Rick’s work was chosen through a Jury process that reviewed nineteen submissions from different artists. Rick is a local glass artist who has twenty five years of experience and many projects installed throughout Canada. Rick enjoys working with contemporary materials and has extensive experience working with glass as an art medium. Please visit Rick Silas’ web site www.coldbentglass.com for more information about his work.

Rick’s proposal for the public art is to etch the large glass wall panels that separate the arts studios from the public hallway with a forest scene that includes the native vegetation of the local area. This work will compliment the open viewable concept of the building designed by Hughes Condon Marler Architects. The open concept and Rick’s glass etching art will provide an opportunity to promote and inspire more people to take part in the arts activities.

Rick will be creating his work of art right at the Arts Centre during the month of August 2011. Please see below the schedule for the public art installation.
