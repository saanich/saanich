Good Neighbours

The importance of historic houses and neighbourhoods to our communities is a theme that many people can relate to. While ornate historic houses are what many people think of first when they hear “heritage”, established, stable and attractive neighbourhoods make an equally important contribution to the community, creating a positive sense of well being, enjoyment, and security.

The theme explores the character and warmth of historic homes, and the timeless appeal of established, older neighbourhoods with vintage house styles, gardens, landscaping, trees and boulevards. Often close to the town centre and developed in an era when the automobile was a less dominant feature of daily life, older neighbourhoods lend themselves to a more sustainable lifestyle. Neighbourhoods and districts from earlier eras also promote a layered sense of community history and contribute to visual appeal and variety. They enhance community attractiveness and livability.

Confirmation of the broad appeal of these qualities is reflected in the desirability of heritage homeownership, the premium prices generally paid, and the fact that heritage homes hold their value better than other segments of the residential market. The value of the individual heritage home is significantly affected by its context of other well preserved vintage homes – the market value lies as much with the character of the neighbourhood as with the house itself. There is also the role of heritage homeowners who through their pride of ownership, stewardship, and investment, give a lot to the community.

 

Earthquake Home Retrofit Class

Fairfield Community Place 
1330 Fairfield Rd
Date: Monday, January 28, 2013
Time: 7:00 to 9:00 pm
Free

In Victoria's four core municipalities there are over 27,000 homes constructed before the simplest earthquake resistance standards were implemented.  Construction prior to 1980 lacked the basic attachment hardware which helps keep the building on its foundation during an earth quake. Modern techniques have been developed to allow homeowners to retrofit their homes and improve earthquake resistance. These simple steps could help you keep your home on its foundation and you in your home.

Download the information sheet

The Earthquake Home Retrofit Class is suited to all skill levels and requires simple, readily available tools and hardware. Take this class and improve your home's ability to withstand earthquakes.
  

Heritage Conservation Management Plan - ONLINE Open House

For the Craigflower Manor and Craigflower Schoolhouse

The public were invited to an Open House on October 4, 2012 from 3:00pm to 7:00pm at Town Hall to discuss the Craigflower Manor & Schoolhouse Management Plan. The Online Open House is below.

A Management Plan is being prepared for the Craigflower Manor & Schoolhouse and the cultural landscapes of their sites flanking the Gorge and the new Craigflower Bridge. The Plan is a joint effort of the Town of View Royal, the BC Heritage Branch, and the District of Saanich.

In August, the Town of View Royal held a Workshop with a number of interested stakeholders to generate ideas for the future of these heritage sites.

The Open House on October 4th will present background on the two sites and their history and alternative directions for their future use and management based on input from the first Workshop.

Heritage Week - February 20-26, 2012

BC Heritage Week is celebrated in communities throughout the province every year, commencing on national Heritage Day, the third Monday in February. Heritage Week themes recognize community heritage buildings, places and history, organize special events and celebrations, and forge partnerships with local business interests. The Theme for the 2012 Heritage Week is "Energy in B.C., A Powerful Past, A Sustainable Future".

Several events are being held at the Saanich Centennial Library to celebrate BC Heritage Week.
