Introduction

In November 2012 the District of Saanich held 3 public open houses so that residents and stakeholders could review and provide feedback, using a survey, on the ideas and concepts contained in the Shelbourne Valley Action Plan Transportation Study and the Shelbourne Valley Land Use and Urban Design Study.  A virtual or online open house was also made available between November 16th and December 17th, 2012, so residents could review the information and complete the survey online.

The ideas, concepts, and the feedback received through the survey, will be used to prepare the first draft of the Shelbourne Valley Action Plan. The policies and recommendations of the draft Plan will be presented for further public review and feedback once completed.

An estimated 400-500 people attended the 3 open houses.  334 surveys were completed; 97 in hard copy and 237 online.

Analysis

After each question is a pie chart or bar graph showing a summary of all responses to the question.  Not all of the respondents answered every question.  The number of respondents who did answer a specific question is shown in brackets ( ) after each question.

Cross tabulations relevant to each question are provided under each question.  For example, a number of questions are cross tabbed according to where the respondent lives: in the Study Area; in Saanich but not the Study Area, and in another municipality.  Other cross tabs are based on the mode of transportation most often used by the respondent when travelling through the Shelbourne Valley: walking, cycling, transit and private motor vehicle.

Posters from the Open Houses illustrating what the question is referring to are also included.
