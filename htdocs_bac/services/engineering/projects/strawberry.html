Update - May 4, 2010

Saanich Municipal Council has deliberated on the Interurban-Wilkinson project and has decided not to implement major changes at this time. It was accepted that the monies related to this project should be used at or near the intersection. These funds are development related (not tax payer funds) and must be used primarily to improve traffic operations at the Interurban-Wilkinson intersection.

In doing so, Saanich will move forward with plans to implement the following:

  1. Provide a concrete median on the Interurban approach to Wilkinson from the stop bar past the gas bar and Knockanback Grill driveway to prevent illegal left turn movement into or out of these driveways.
  2. Provide a right turn bay on both Interurban approaches to Wilkinson at the most advantageous time (when redevelopment or rezoning occurs), or when funds become available.
  3. Provide a sidewalk on the north side of Interurban from Roy Road to the existing accessible bus stop to the east. This will allow the removal of the bus stop on Interurban westbound at Roy Road.
  4. Provide bike lane markings where available and in conjunction with any new designs.

The Municipality of Saanich is committed to balanced transportation and continues to look for ways to improve transit service to this area.

The sidewalk along Santa Anita is a separate, though related, project and will be constructed in 2010 to provide a complete pedestrian sidewalk from Hastings to Wilkinson.

Saanich is currently reviewing some key items that were voiced at the latest community meetings. These include: a review of time restricted closure of Hastings through and left approach; construction phasing to leave some items unconstructed until necessary (if at all), review how large vehicles will get through the detour routes (dump trucks with trailers (60ft long), trucks with horse trailers, etc). Decisions will be made by the end of the month and they will be posted here as well as sent to the Community Association and residents via representatives.
