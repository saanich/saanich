About

Cook Street is a 0.9km major road within the District of Saanich, providing access to several commercial sites at the northwest end and residential properties southwards. This section of Cook Street is within the Quadra Local Area Plan - a plan that will help guide the future of this neighbourhood. Based on this plan, the overall "community vision" is one that is vibrant and inclusive for drivers, cyclists and pedestrians; one that is safe, providing a continuous network of routes connecting neighbourhoods, parks, trails, commercial and institutional facilities; and one that protects and enhances urban forests, wildlife and parks.

The new design of Cook Street will meet the needs of motorists, cyclists and pedestrians of all ages, as well as reducing greenhouse gas emissions and improving storm water quality. The new design will improve safety, be more inviting for all users of the Cook Street corridor and reflect the neighbourhood vision as defined in the Quadra Local Area Plan.

Please visit this page in the future for more information on this project. The District of Saanich will update this page when project milestones, concept designs and open house dates become finalized.

The comments from the 2nd public open house will aid in furthering the Detail Design of the project. Refer to the Reports & Concept Plans page for 50% Design Drawing excerpts provided at the open house.

10/22/2012 - Cook Street Open House Follow-Up - COMPLETE

With the public open house for the Concept Design of Cook Street complete, please fill out the Online Survey Form by Friday, November 9, 2012. The comments will be collected and will aid in the concept design of Cook Street.

10/12/2012 - Notice of Open House, October 22, 2012 - COMPLETE

The District of Saanich will be hosting a public open house for the Conceptual Design of Cook Street on October 22, 2012 at the Cedar Hill Golf Course Banquet Room (1400 Derby Road) from 4:00PM to 8:00PM.

We wish to present various complete street concepts at this meeting and gather feedback on each presented.  The comments gathered at this open house will aid in the concept design of Cook Street before the project proceeds to detailed design. We look forward in working with you as we move forward with this project.

 

10/09/2012 - Sounding Board at Thrifty Foods - COMPLETE

With the help of Thrifty Foods, the District of Saanich have installed a sounding board outside of their Cook Street store. As sounding boards are meant to be interactive to the public, this location was chosen in an effort to expose the project to the most people local to Cook Street, onsite.

Please post your comments onto the board with the supplied paper and writing material. The District of Saanich staff will monitor these boards daily and collect the data to help with the conceptual design of Cook Street.
