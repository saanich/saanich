In 2003 Municipal staff developed a concept plan for Tattersall Drive between Vernon Avenue and Quadra Street.  This concept plan, supported by the community and endorsed by Council in 2004, was used to guide the design for the Tattersall Drive Upgrade Project.

The Tattersall Drive Upgrade Project includes: new underground utilities, sidewalks, bike lanes and rain gardens.  These upgrades support universal accessibility, sustainable transportation, and will reduce storm water runoff into nearby water courses.

Tattersall Drive is a major road which provides a primary connection between Quadra Street, the Pat Bay Highway, the Trans Canada Highway, and the new Uptown Development.  Tattersall is an aging road that is in need of a major rehabilitation.  The underground utilities on Tattersall are also reaching the end of their functional life and are in need of being replaced.

This upgrade project will include the replacement of wood-stave storm drains, the widening and rehabilitation of the road surface, enhancement of the intersection of Tattersall Drive and Calumet Avenue, and the replacement of sewer and water mains.  The completion of the road will include landscaped boulevards and parking bays to enhance the appearance of the road and to accommodate residents' desire for on-street parking.  The implementation of bike lanes, sidewalks and rain gardens continues to support the Municipality's goal of developing a sustainable community.
