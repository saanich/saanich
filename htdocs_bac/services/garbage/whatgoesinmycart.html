What goes in my organics recycling cart?

What type of food-soiled paper can I add to my kitchen scraps recycling?

Food-soiled paper can be added to your organics recycling cart as long as it contains no plastic. Things like napkins, tissues without human waste, waxed paper, cardboard and muffin liners can be added.

However, plastic coated paper (like some coffee cups and meat liners) cannot be added. If you are not sure what you have, try tearing the paper - plastic coated paper will stretch. Remember if your paper is clean, recycling it in your blue box.

Keeping Your Kitchen Cotnainer and Organics Recycling Cart Clean and Using Compostable Bags

You do not have to line your kitchen scraps container or organics recycling cart. However, using a compostable bag or paper liner will help keep your kitchen container and organics recycling clean and reduce odour.

If you wish to line your kitchen container and organics recycling cart, you can use compostable bags, newspaper or paper products like paper towels and paper bags.

Large and small compostable bags and paper bag liners for containers and carts are available at grocery stores, drug stores and home hardware stores.

See how to make your own kitchen container liner from newspaper or watch the video!

Why can't I use plastic or biodegradable bags?

Plastic and biodegradable bags contain plastic and cannot be processed with your organics into compost. If plastic or biodegradable bags are used, the organics recycling will not be collected because it cannot be recycled or taken to the landfill because of the food scraps ban.

How do I know I’m using compostable bags?

Compostable bags and paper bag liners are available in many sizes and at many local retailers. Look for the certified Compostable logo to ensure you are purchasing bags which can be composted with your organics recycling (kitchen scraps and yard trimmings).
