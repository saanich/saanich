Yard Trimmings will be collected too!

A great new feature of Greener Garbage Collection is that yard trimmings can be added to the organics recycling cart for curbside collection every second week.

You can top up your organics recycling cart with non-invasive yard trimmings (grass clippings, prunings, plants and branches no larger than 5cm in diameter). Soil, sod, ashes, rocks, tree root balls, invasive species, animal waste or wood waste will not be accepted. Please make sure everything fits in your cart and the lid closes properly.

If you have more yard trimmings than will fit in the cart, save them for your next collection day or bring them to the Garden Recycling Drop-off at the Public Works Yard. Check the Garden Recycling Drop-off web camera to avoid waiting in line and for our hours of operation.

What about using my backyard composter?

If you have a backyard compost, please continue to use it as it is the most cost-effective and environmentally friendly way to dispose of food waste and non-invasive yard trimmings. Kitchen scraps recycling complements backyard composting by accepting a wider range of food waste that may not be suitable for backyard composting such as cooked food, meat, bones, fish, baked goods, food soiled paper and food packaging.

Backyard composting yard trimmings can provide vital nutrients to your lawn and gardens and excess leaves can be raked to the curb during the Leaf Collection Program every fall.

Please remember, do NOT place any plastics in your organics recycling cart. They can’t be composted.
