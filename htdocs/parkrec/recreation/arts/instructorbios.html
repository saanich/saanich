Ceramics

Nancy Alexander

From Nancy's early days starting out with the Penticton Potters Guild, she has developed and refined her throwing and glazing techniques by taking courses, participating in workshops and of course, years and years of practice.

As an educator Nancy brings many years of experience and skills for encouraging learning. She has been an invited artist-in-residence in schools andat the Hotel Grand Pacific.Nancy has organized and participated in numerous showsandexhibitions, including the Saanich West Artists Studio Tour.

Paige Coull

Paige Coull grew up in the Ottawa Valley, Ontario. Her pursuit of an education in art took her first to Montreal, where creating work in many different mediums, she found her passion in ceramics. She moved to Calgary, Alberta where she completed a Bachelor of Fine Arts Degree at the Alberta College of Art and Design in 2004, majoring in ceramic arts. With access there to a variety of studios and media, she began incorporating textiles, alternative photography, glass blowing, print media and jewellery into her pottery.

She has taught a range of classes for beginners and experienced potters alike.

Ron Douglas

Ron studied ceramics at the Kootenay School of Art in the seventies and established his first pottery studio in 1972 in Edmonton Alberta, where he owned and operated Handworks Gallery. In Victoria, Ron Douglas operates the Creekside Pottery Studio in the scenic Oak Bay area. In his studio Ron produces a wide range of work that is high fired, crafted stoneware as well as earthenware pottery decorated with a colourful Majolica (Italian) style. The work ranges from one of a kind decorative sculpture to functional pottery for everyday use.

He has been a production potter and instructor of studio ceramics for over three decades; ten of those years have been with the Cedar Hill Recreation Centre. Ron believes in the innate creativity of the individual. His passion and dedication to the craft are evident in his art work and teachings.

Michael Giles

Michael made his first pot in 1975, igniting a lifelong passion for pottery. He studied with ceramicists Wayne Ngan, Simon Henson and Walter Dexter and been influenced by the traditions of Chinese and Japanese pottery. He employs several techniques, sometimes combining wheel-thrown and hand-built elements into a single pot. For Michael, the joy of ceramics comes not from producing a “perfect” pot but from true engagement in the act of creating the work. This passion for the process comes through in his teaching.

Michael began teaching at Cedar Hill ten years ago; the Arts Centre’s new ceramics studio is a “potter’s dream,” and a wonderful place to teach.

Suzanne Page

For many years Suzanne Page has taught all levels and ages of pottery classes with Saanich Recreation.  She has taught beginner and intermediate pottery for adults, kid’s classes, glazing and design, sculpture and children’s summer camp. Suzanne began her ceramics studies in Alberta in the 1980’s and continues to explore her unique pottery and sculptural pursuits. Suzanne exhibits her unique sculptural forms locally and internationally along with her thrown pottery and Raku often incorporating themes from her first-nation’s heritage.

Dance

Marielle & Mohamed Andre Duranteau ~ Wontanara Drum & Dance

Andre is a professional Master dancer, choreographer and musician from Guinea, West Africa. He developed his skills as a singer, drummer and dancer, travelling and performing around the country and spent 10 years with "Le Ballet Taille,” where he performed as lead dancer and received a nationally accredited diploma. Andre has over 10 years experience teaching dance to individuals and groups from around the globe. Marielle studied various dance forms for 10 years prior to discovering and focusing on African dance. Her study of African dance led her to Guinea, West Africa where she learned from Guinean dancer N'nato Camara and other renowned artists and performers. During her BFA studies in Europe, she studied and performed African dance in both Dublin and Paris. Since 2007, Mareille has resided in Victoria, sharing and teaching her knowledge of Guinean Dance.

In 2008, Marielle and Andre established the local West African Drum and Dance troupe, Wontanara Drum & Dance. Together they have delivered exceptional performances and classes throughout the region for people of all ages and abilities. They have performed for diverse audiences at numerous shows, festivals and fundraising events around Vancouver Island. They specialize in sharing and teaching traditional dances, rhythms and songs originating from Guinea, West Africa. Their programs enhance artistic expression, health and well-being while raising awareness of cultural diversity and building community.

Allison Frith

Allison has been dancing since the age of five, taking classes in ballet, jazz, modern, and tap. Her training has included successful completion of RAD Ballet and ISTD Modern Theatre examinations at the Intermediate level. Allison has performed extensively in local shows and Festivals, including Alberta Ballet's production of the Nutcracker, and works by modern choreographer Nicole McSkimming. Allison has been teaching classes for Wendy Steen Mitchell Dance and Cedar Hill Recreation Centre for the past 8 years, and her dances have won awards at both the Greater Victoria Performing Arts Festival and the DanceWorks Festival. Allison also recently graduated from UVic, earning a Bachelor of Science degree in Kinesiology.

Jenna Holmes

Jenna began her dance career through Cedar Hill Recreation Centre at the age of four. She advanced to Pacific Dance Centre where she trained intensively in ballet for eleven years. She has extensive performing experience, having taken part in the Greater Victoria Performing Arts Festival and Galas for many years, as well as a variety of studio recitals. During university, Jenna began dancing in the adult ballet class at Cedar Hill, and now is pleased to have the opportunity to teach it. Jenna also teaches and volunteers in an assortment of other classes and loves passing on her knowledge of dance to people of all ages.

Elizabeth Tinga

Liz began dancing with Wendy Steen Mitchell Dance! at the age of 7. Liz trained in jazz under Excalibre Dance and Dansko Studios. She has performed and competed in the Greater Victoria Performing Arts Festival and Gala, in Danceworks Festival and Gala, in 5,6,7,8 Showtime and in the musicals "Little Shop of Horrors" and "West Side Story". Liz has 15 years teaching experience and continues to expand her choreographic styles in jazz and lyrical. She has completed the Royal Academy of Dance pre-primary in dance and primary in dance training course and has extensive knowledge and teaching experience of the RAD syllabus grades pre-primary through intermediate. Liz continues to be inspired by her students to advance her own dance training as well as to share her love of this art with people of all ages and abilities.

Kelsey Dukelow ~ Boston Dance Collective

Kelsey Dukelow has been taking tap, jazz, and funk training since the age of 4. She began dancing in Winnipeg, Manitoba before coming to Victoria and BroadStreet Dance in 1994. As a young teen, Kelsey assisted the preschool dancers at BroadStreet Dance and taught at various studios and recreation centers in Victoria. Visual Arts

Anita Brunckhurst

Anita is passionate about Art and Science. Drawing on her studies in Anatomy, Physiology, Biology and Chemistry she enjoys designing programs for children and adults that blend science with art by creating opportunities for genuine discovery inside and outside of the classroom.

Anita is a full juried member of the Island Illustrators Society. Her paintings have been exhibited in the Sidney and Sooke Fine Art Shows and the Sooke Harbour House Gallery. Anita is regularly commissioned to paint murals, still-life, portraits and illustrations. Working mostly in acrylics, she likes having opportunities to use her art to bring people together... to emphasize, celebrate and share the things we have in common.

Douglas Galbraith

Doug has always worked in oils but has recently ventured into acrylic because of its immediacy. Most of the work he creates is a reaction to nature. Living on Vancouver Island offers “limitless sources of beauty of a grandeur seldom seen.” Doug studied drawing and paintingin school, university and as a classical composition apprentice. He also holds a diploma in Graphic Design.

“I move between realistic and abstract ways of interpretation because I want to recognize but also penetrate. I try to balance image with decorative spaces within the canvas, sometimes with gold leaf flakes. I do this because I believe a painting is, after all, ornamentation for our environment. I want my canvasses to contain juxtaposing environments that reflect those of our world.”

Dixie MacUisdin

Dixie’s love of painting began young, inspired by the Ottawa Valley landscape. She earned BFA from Mount Allison University specializing in painting and photography. She regularly shows her work through the Coast Collective Art Centre, where she is an Associate Member.

Dixie works primarily in acrylic and watercolour. The beauty of our local landscape inspires Dixie as she explores the versatility of her chosen mediums and experiments with new techniques in her work. She shares her love and knowledge of painting by instructing classes at various locations in Victoria. For her, it is a rewarding endeavour to explore creative expression within in a group setting.

Natalie Shumka

Natalie has been an art instructor for over ten years. She has developed a teaching style that focuses on each and every student in her class. Each participant receives personalized instruction on technique. Questions relating to colour, composition, and basic "how to" are answered with enthusiasm. Natalie encourages each student to discover their style, develop ideas for their next project, and build confidence to start and finish.

Natalie graduated with a Diploma of Fine Arts in drawing and painting from the Victoria College of Art. She has taught theraputic art as well as classes for early childhood and school age children, as well as for adults. Natalie's artworks are in private collections in Canada and the United States, and she has shown extensively throughout Victoria and the Lower Mainland.

Andrea Soos

Andrea is a professional artist and art instructor who has taught for over 17 years. Her experience includes teaching children of all ages as well as adults. Andrea has practiced and taught in a wide range of media including printmaking, mixed media, encaustics, sculpture, mosaics, painting, drawing and cartooning and mixed media. Recently Andrea has been exploring collage and wax in both 2D and 3D. She holds a Bachelor of Fine Arts and a minor in Arts in Education from the University of Victoria.

Stacey Van Drunen

My main goal is for students to have tons of fun while learning the basics of art, drawing and design! I am experienced in teaching the fundamentals of art to students of all ages. I strongly believe that my role as an art teacher is to encourage my students to be creative, believe that they can do whatever they set their minds to and create worlds that are just as unique and special as they are! This means that I'm really easygoing about how my students go about applying the techniques that I teach them. I will teach them different concepts and tools that will help them harness different aspects of drawing and art, but they are completely free to take those things wherever their creative minds lead them! I also strongly believe in individualized teaching and coaching. The bottom line is that art should always be FUN and I make every effort to keep it that way, but still with a lesson plan and a general goal in mind!
