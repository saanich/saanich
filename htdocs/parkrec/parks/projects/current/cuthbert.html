Overview

This park is named in honour of Major H. Cuthbert Holmes (1890 - 1968). Major Holmes was a leading advocate of planning for future public amenities throughout the Capital District, and was especially interested in preserving and protecting greenbelt lands along the Colquitz River. The park was developed in the late 1960’s and early 1970’s and maintained to a level consistent with a natural park. In 1986 the park was turned over to Saanich Parks, and in 1987 a significant portion of land was granted to the Provincial Capital Commission and subsequently leased to Saanich.

The area commonly referred to as Cuthbert Holmes Park is actually a combination of Cuthbert Holmes Park (Municipal Park classification) and Tillicum Park (Community Park classification). Together they form 25.7 ha (63.5 acres) of park located in the Tillicum Local Area. For the purpose of this report this collective area is referred to as Cuthbert Holmes Park. Approx. 12 ha (30 acres) is owned by the Provincial Capital Commission and leased to Saanich for 99 years, expiring in 2086. The road right-of-ways of the Trans Canada Highway and Admirals Road function as part of the park, as does a small parcel (.05ha/.13 acre) owned by the BC Transit Financial Authority. The park contains important local and regional trail connections, a portion of the Colquitz River, and a variety of ecosystems and wildlife.

Process

June 13, 2012 - Open House

Saanich Parks held its third public open house June 13, 2012 to build on information gathered over the past 2 years, and further inform the park’s Management Plan. When complete the plan will include a concept plan and management practices to achieve a responsible balance of recreation and ecological protection.

This plan must address a number of inter-related factors that have negatively affected this Natural Park. Unrestrained access and unauthorized trails have resulted in soil compaction, loss of soil and plant understory, habitat degradation, and contributed to the spread of invasive plants. Areas heavily infested by invasive plants, and certain isolated pockets, harbour negative activities which discourage use. The lack of definition and facilities for appropriate activities has led to user conflicts and environmental damage in some areas.

We’ll be exploring how to define trails and identify appropriate activities, how to protect and restore sensitive areas, how to discourage negative activities, and how to accommodate legitimate users including nature lovers, families, dog-walkers, joggers and bicyclists.

The plan is intended to protect ecological resources, balance a variety of activities, and improve public safety.  This third public open house builds on the information we’ve gathered during the last two years, including public input, Saanich policies, and environmental review. We are getting closer to finalizing the management plan for the park, and are interested in receiving community feedback.

June 13, 2012 - Open House

There was a good turn out at the park’s third Open House, with 87 people signing in and a number of others passing through. There was a lot of discussion and opinions were voiced, including 84 completed surveys and a number of independent submissions.

The on-line survey was available from June 14 to July 8, 2012. The following are the general results of the survey, a link to more detailed results is given below (respondents’ names and comments are not shown).

  • The Park Vision received 64% support.
  • Goals were generally supported: Natural Areas Management - 86%, Trails – 84%, Recreational Opportunities – 64%, and Safety and Security 87%.
  • Voluntary compliance to keep walkers, bikes and pets to trails in sensitive areas was supported 83%, 83% and 68% respectively.
  • 37% supported an active recreation facility in an area outside high sensitivity areas. Of these 53% supported a bike skills facility, 44% supported a playground, and 50% supported an informal play space.
  • The main reasons for visiting the park were to walk a dog – 67%, run – 18%, walk – 62%, appreciate nature – 60%, commute – 23%, bike ride – 24%, nd ‘other’ – 7%.
  • 83% of respondents live within a 15 minute walk of Cuthbert Homes Park.

Next Steps

Community input will be summarized on the District of Saanich website. When completed, the final draft of the management plan will be taken to advisory committees and Saanich Council for endorsement and approval. If approved the plan’s recommendations will be implemented over the next 10 years, starting in 2013.
