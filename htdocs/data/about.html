What is Saanich Open Data all about?

The District of Saanich’s Open Data initiative seeks to enhance the way citizens obtain, view and understand public information. It involves publishing “datasets” which are essentially collections of data, often presented in tabular form. While most of this information is already available on websites or in print, open data involves machine-readable formats that can be put into spreadsheets or used to create useful applications. This makes information easier to consume, combine and interpret.

How can I get Saanich data?

The District of Saanich has provided the initial sponsorship in partnership with the Municipalities of Nanaimo and North Cowichan to create an Open Data Catalogue for all Vancouver Island municipalities.

This catalogue will be available in late 2012 and become the primary method for Saanich to publish and citizens to access this information. Initially formed under the Vancouver Island IT (VIIT) banner and now in partnership with CivicInfo BC, this catalogue will ultimately become available to all municipalities in the province of BC with Saanich, Nanaimo and North Cowichan as the first government participants.

For more information about Saanich’s participation in Open Data, please email us at opendata@saanich.ca.

While the CivicInfo catalog is under development, Saanich will make “raw” dataset files available on this website.

Why Open Data?

The purpose of releasing Open Data is to:

  • Enhance citizen engagement
  • Improve operational efficiencies
  • Support economic growth
  • Support Open Government & transparency
