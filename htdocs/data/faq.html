What is open data?

Open Data is a philosophy and practice of making non-personal information available to the public that is:

  • Free
  • In machine-readable format

Why is Saanich involved?

Saanich is responding to the world-wide Open Data movement in which governments at all levels are answering citizens’ demands to publish information in a format that is much easier to receive, analyze and understand. There are several key benefits that are offered by adopting open data including:

  • Citizen engagement is improved as citizens and businesses are able to combine and analyze data to get a deeper understanding of issues and lead to innovation in how residents interact with government.
  • Operational efficiencies reduce the "distribution" cost for many kinds of information.
  • Economic growth is stimulated as businesses and services are created and bureaucracy to obtain information and conduct business is reduced .
  • Promotion of Open Government & transparency where barriers to information are greatly reduced.

Who is involved in Open Data?

Open Data is created, managed, delivered and consumed by:

  • Governments (Federal, Provincial and Local): primary publishers of Open Data.
  • Citizens: study, combine and analyze data for personal interest; may consume products and services created by businesses that use open data.
  • Businesses: use Open Data as an input in their products and services; may use Open Data to improve decision-making.
  • Academic institutions: study, combine, analyze and publish Open Data for research and academic purposes.
  • Software developers: a citizen with technical skills who uses Open Data with the intent to form a company and provide products and services.
