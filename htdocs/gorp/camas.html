Site Description

Camas Park is a small remnant Garry oak ecosystem bordered by McKenzie Ave to the south and homes along Camas Court to the north.  An open camas meadow is surrounded to the north by oak trees and native shrubs, with a rocky outcrop on the west side of the site.

Area: approx. 0.34 ha

Elevation: 35-41m

Aspect: crest of hill

Moisture regime: subxeric to mesic

There are two distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Grassland over Shallow Soils

Mature Woods over Moderate Soils

History

The site appears to have been open shrubland adjacent to savanna with farms nearby from 1888 until, at least, the 1950’s.  In 1969, McKenzie Avenue was constructed along the south of the meadow.  The site has recently been officially re-zoned as Park after the bike lane and sidewalk expansion.

Restoration

Camas Park including removal of Scotch broom, Himalayan blackberry, English ivy, daphne, invasive grasses and weedy species such as cyclamen and nipplewort.  Cutting back of native snowberry in some areas of the meadow has occurred to keep the meadow open.  Early in the project, attempts to discourage the invasive grasses included laying leaf mulch over the meadow in the fall. 

Some native flora species commonly found at Camas Park:

Quercus garryana Garry oak
Symphoricarpos albus snowberry
Oemleria cerasiformis Indian plum
Camassia quamash common camas
Brodiaea coronaria harvest brodiaea
Brodiaea hyacinthina fool’s onion
Sanicula crassicaulis Pacific sanicle

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At Camas Park some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including: Coast Capital Savings Real Estate and Reynolds Secondary Leadership Club.

Camas Park is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
