Site Description

Located on the west side of Mt. Douglas Park, best accessed from the Mercer trailhead at Blenkinsop Road.  This site is on the mid to upper section of the south facing slope.  The main trail runs along the bottom of the site, with trails up to the crest of the slope delineating the other project boundaries.    

Area:

Elevation: 100 – 130 m

Aspect: 140°

Moisture regime: xeric

There is one distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Grassland over Shallow Soils

History

In 1843 Mt. Douglas was logged by the Hudson’s Bay Company to supply timber for Fort Victoria.  By 1858, the area became a government reserve.  Many of the existing trails in the Park today were created and used by flocks of sheep through the late 1800’s.  On September 30, 1889, thanks to Sir James Douglas, the Province of British Columbia granted Mount Douglas to the Corporation of the City of Victoria “upon trust to maintain and preserve the same as a public park or pleasure ground for the use, recreation and enjoyment of the public”.

Restoration

Restoration at Little Mt. Doug has included removal and control of invasive species, especially Scotch broom.   This site has more of an intact species composition compared to other GORP sites and has the highest diversity of native grasses.  Restoration and management at this site also includes considerations of human impacts such as from mountain bikes and other issues.

Some native flora species commonly found at the Little Mt. Doug GORP Site include:

Quercus garryana Garry oak
Arbutus menziesii Arbutus
Camassia leichtlinii great camas
Camassia quamash common camas
Brodiaea coronaria harvest brodiaea
Brodiaea hyacinthina fool’s onion
Allium acuminatum Hooker’s onion
Sanicula crassicaulis Pacific sanicle
Polypodium glycyrrhiza licorice fern

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At Little Mt. Doug some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including: Friends of Mt. Doug, Claremont Secondary and the John Howard Society.

Mt. Douglas Park is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
