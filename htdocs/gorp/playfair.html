Site Description

This site is located in Playfair Park on the north and northwest sides of the park from the Rock Street Parking lot to the rocky outcrop and bench near the garden border at Cumberland Road.  This site is the most intact GORP site with spectacular spring wildflower blooms.  Access is via Rock Street or Cumberland Road.

Area: GORP site is approx. 0.8 ha

Elevation: 50 – 56 m

Aspect: 34°

Moisture regime: mesic

There are two distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Rock Outcrops and Thin Soils

Open Woods over Deep Soils

History

An 1888 map shows this area as intact oak savanna.  By the turn of the century, there was a dairy farm adjacent to the site.  In 1924 Playfair became a park with undeveloped woods to the south and southeast.  Development around the park continued to increase through the next decades.  In 1958 Playfair became one of the official parks of the new Saanich Parks system and part of this park was developed into an arboretum for rhododendrons. 

Restoration

Restoration at the Playfair Park GORP site has unfolded in a very careful manner due to the sensitivity of the site.  Invasive plant species, especially orchard grass (Dactylis glomerata) are the most challenging threats for the site.  Removal of English ivy and Himalayan blackberry has been occurring in the west part of the site.  Currently, a partnership project with special funding is ongoing to address the invasive grasses including assessment, planning, restoration and monitoring.  Human impacts to the site are also being managed and a split rail fence was recently constructed along the southern border.

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site. 

Playfair Park is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
