Site Description

Located across from Rithet’s Bog, this site is bordered by Chatterton Way, the Pat Bay Hwy, an office building and a townhouse complex.  This rocky hill is one of the more intact GORP sites and has a lovely view of Mt. Douglas and Rithet’s Bog from the top.  A trail starts at the sidewalk on Chatterton Way and extends north towards the townhouses and west, up the hill. 

Area: Park approximate. 0.8 ha, total restoration area approximate 1.3 ha

Elevation: 18-36m

Aspect: mounded hill

Moisture regime: xeric to mesic

There are four distinct biophysical zones at this site, described in our soil mapping as:

Grassland over Shallow Soils

Mixed Vegetation and Soils

Mature Woods over Moderate Soils

Grassland over Moderate Soils

History

By 1956 Rithet’s Bog was cleared and used was farmland with increasing rural density on the opposite side of the road.  The suburban development of the Broadmead area began in the 1960’s.  Farming operations ended around 1993 and the bog site was given to the Municipality of Saanich in 1995 as parkland by the Guinness family.  Chatterton Hill Park has remnants of an old fence along the western edge.

Restoration

Invasive species management has been a key activity at Chatterton Hill Park including removal of Scotch broom, Himalayan blackberry, English hawthorne, poison hemlock, thistles and invasive grasses. Restoration has also included replanting appropriate native vegetation in specific areas and seasonal cutting of invasive plants and grasses.  Managing the impacts of human use is a significant issue at this site and includes directing visitors to a new trail that lessens trampling and other impacts to the ecosystem.

Stewardship

GORP Volunteers, led by the Coordinator of Volunteers and Saanich staff, conduct restoration and stewardship activities on the site.  At Chatterton Hill some organizations and clubs have participated in stewardship including the Coast Capital Savings Youth Team.

Chatterton Hill is within the Saanich Municipal Parks system.
