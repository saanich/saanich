Community Gardens can offer residents a place to socialize; a place to get fresh air and exercise; a place to feel accomplishment; and most importantly a place to grow their own food.  Community Gardens are important tools towards success in reducing our carbon foot print by allowing those who choose to live in urban areas the opportunity to spend some time working with nature and by growing food they reduce their reliance on transported produce.

Community Gardens Policy

Saanich Policy defines a “community garden” as a site operated by volunteers and:

  • Used for the production of produce for the personal use
  • Where instructional programming may be offered
  • Where plots and services are provided to members in exchange for a fee.

Saanich’s Community Garden Policy recognizes the need for community gardens and outlines ways that the Municipality will support existing gardens and the establishment of future community gardens.

New Community Gardens being planned!

The Parks and Recreation Department have been working with two community groups to explore the possibility of establishing two new community garden sites, one in Gorge Park and one behind Saanich Commonwealth Place.  An update on progress was recently provided by staff to Chair Gerrard and the Parks, Trails and Recreation Committee.

Staff will be undertaking consultations with the community in both areas  in the near future and should there be support for these potential sites, work will continue towards establishing operating agreements with local groups interested in operating the garden sites. Further information can be found on the Parks page here.

Community gardens have existed in Saanich for over 30 years.  These two community gardens are further supported by the District of Saanich with water supply and road access.  The gardens are managed by separate community groups as listed below.

Agnes Street Gardens

Agnes Street Gardens has been in continuous operation for over 30 years.  It is managed by the Agnes Street Gardener’s Association.  There are approximately 80 plots (some shared) 90 sq. metres (1,000 sq. feet) in size. Visitors are welcome.  Gardeners rent plots for the calendar year and choose what garden plants to grow.  The garden is located at 613 Agnes Street.

Capital City Allotment Association

These allotment gardens have existed since the 1970s. They are located on land between the Pat Bay Highway and Carey Road with more than 140 plots (some shared as half plots) that are typically 90 sq. metres (1,000 sq. feet) in size.

The Victoria Native Friendship Centre

This new garden located at the Friendship Centre was renently built by a group of more than 30 volunteers.  The garden produces vegetables and fruits for the centre's programs. Some of the produce is distributed through an emergency food fund for families in need, and a portion is given to the volunteers who maintain the garden. There is also a greenhouse.

Greater Victoria Compost Education Centre

The GVCEC is a non-profit organization, with charitable status located in the Fernwood neighbourhood, providing composting and organic gardening education to CRD residents.

Grow a Row Organization

Grow a Row distributes extra produce from local gardens to local people who can use it.  Gardeners sign up to donate a row of food from their garden to local food banks and nutrition programs.

http://vicgrowarow.org

Seniors Garden Program

The District of Saanich manages one plot at the Capital City allotment Gardens for a Seniors Program that provides seniors with the opportunity to learn more about gardening and to grow vegetables.

If you are interested in learning more about the opportunities for Seniors at the Capital City Garden Allotments please refer to Saanich's Active Living Guide or call 250-475-5408.

Edible gardens in the Parks

The Parks Department plants edible plants in some of Saanich’s Parks.  These surprises in the gardens inspire people to enjoy a nibble when a crop is ready and inspires people to plant their own gardens for cultivation.
