Buy Local Initiatives

Farm Fresh

A website and directory that connects people to the farms with a map, product list, and a season schedule.

Get Fresh with Locals

This website supports local Vancouver Island farmers, producers and rurally based business by helping you to find the local growers, producers, suppliers and supporters of local food.  They have an online guide or you may pick up a free printed map guide to Saanich Peninsula farms, U-pick, fresh seafood, farmers markets, wineries, organic and hormone-free meat and poultry products, local food suppliers and Victoria to Saanich area restaurants.  Monitor the guide to find out what vegetables are ready and where for this time of year.

The Vancouver Island Diet

The Vancouver Island Diet website is similar to the 100 Mile Diet but, attempts to make the search for local producers and farmers on Vancouver Island easier.  Their Food Producer's page lets you search producers by product, by area or by name.  The data base provides information on where the product is sold and links to a website if available.

BC Farms & Food

BC Farms & Food is a news and information site reporting on food and farming in British Columbia. The website works to increase people's awareness of local food opportunities and its role in our health, happiness, economic development, and security. The site provides maps of some local farms and food distribution sites on Southern Vancouver Island and Salt Spring Island and has articles on local farm and food issues and ideas.

FoodRoots

A not-for-profit co-op distributor of local naturally grown produce and foods processed in our region.

“FoodRoots is the link between growers, processors and you.”

Farm to Cafeteria

A Canada wide initiative called Farm to Cafeteria program is connecting local, nutritious, and sustainably produced foods to public agency cafeterias such as schools, universities, colleges, and hospitals.  Farm to Cafeteria Canada is guided by a 12-member advisory committee of representatives from across the Canada and is currently managed and administered by the Public Health Association of British Columbia (PHABC).

In Saanich, David and Nathalie Chambers of Madrona Farm have been a part of a Food to Schools (F2S) program over the last year where farm produce is provide to Reynolds Secondary School for the students to enjoy a lunch time salad bar once or twice a week

SPUD

Sustainable Produce Urban Delivery is a local delivery supplier of produce and sustainable products from the local food community. "Organic and local food is our passion; we believe access to real, healthy food should be simple and easy, and what we eat should come from sources as close to us as possible."

Saanich Organics

Saanich Organics deliver fresh, healthy, local, certified organic produce to the Great Victoria area. They run a residential box delivery program; sell at the Moss Street Market and the James Bay Market; and provide produce to local restaurants and grocery stores.

Shared Organics

Shared Organics provides home delivery of fresh, organic, closest-to-home produce and groceries to your doorstep. They have been delivering to households in the Greater Victoria area since 1997. Shared organics also offers a pick up option of a ready-packed order or a bulk buying option.

Camosun College

Camosun has taken on the challenge of buying locally-grown food.  Camosun talks about the challenge in a May 2011 article Serving up sustainability – local food for thought and students. “It is not as easy as turning up at a farmer's gate.”… “Eighteen to 20% of the produce purchased for the Camosun ARAMARK locations is grown on Vancouver Island and 72% of it is grown in BC. In addition, 90% of the bakery products are baked in Victoria, and all eggs are BC-raised cage free. Milk and cream are purchased from Island suppliers and the focus is on Canadian-raised protein.”

University of Victoria

UVic has established a policy in their Strategic Plan to "further develop (our) ethical and sustainable purchasing practices" including purchasing local food. The University Food Services search for suppliers that can provide as much local foods as possible and suppliers that source locally prepared food products.

Saanich Parks Horticulture continues Urban Agriculture

Kelly Mulhern, Saanich Parks Horticulture

In 2010 Saanich Park’s Horticulture staff took the initiative of incorporating edibles into a few locations in the municipality. The most notable, Gorge Waterway Park, next to the playground, and the Municipal Hall planters in Middleton Plaza (at the entrance to the Hall from the parking lot.)

At the Gorge, vegetables included corn, scarlet runner beans, tomatoes, pumpkins, cucumbers, sunflowers and even a banana! Hand made signs were produced for each plant which added to the charm, and hopefully the children who visited the playground got a chance to see how their vegetables are grown, while enjoying a snack at the same time.

At the Municipal Hall we planted tomatoes and a selection of herbs in the planters which typically would have flowers. We replaced some of the hanging baskets with baskets of juicy strawberries cascading down. In 2011, we added a vegetable patch at the entrance to the Municipal Hall off Darwin Ave.

For the 2012 season, we have continued the tradition and have again planted the playground bed at the Gorge Waterway and the planters on Middleton Plaza at the Municipal Hall. Unfortunately, due to a large construction project in the parking lot we were unable to plant the bed at the entrance off Darwin.

Once our crops start producing, we encourage the public and staff to share in the harvest and perhaps start an edible plot in their garden or a planter filled with veggies of their own.
