MAURICE CHAZOTTES TO BE HONOURED WITH

QUEEN ELIZABETH II DIAMOND JUBILEE MEDAL

For Immediate Release                                                                    Date:  Updated on February 21, 2013

SAANICH – Mayor Frank Leonard and Council will bestow the honour of the Queen’s Diamond Jubilee Medal on Mr. Maurice Chazottes.

The Queen Elizabeth II Diamond Jubilee commemorative medal was created to mark the 60th anniversary celebrations of Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II's accession to the Throne. The medal is a tangible way for Canada to honour Her Majesty for her service to this country. At the same time, it serves to honour significant contributions and achievements by Canadians.

Saanich Council is recognizing Mr. Chazottes with the Queen Elizabeth II Diamond Jubilee medal for his outstanding contributions to the region through public service most notably, his work related to the protection of natural areas.

Mr. Chazottes served as the Province’s first full time Chief of Hansard and was responsible for initiating the first daily Hansard record. After retirement, Mr. Chazottes was elected to Municipal Council for the District of North Saanich. He was also a Capital Regional District Board member where he was instrumental in obtaining support for a Regional Parks’ Land Acquisition Fund. Since the establishment of the Land Acquisition Fund in 2000, 4,485 hectares of land have been acquired. Previously Mr. Chazottes was recognized with the EcoStar Community Environmental Award for his work to preserve natural areas.

Mr. Chazottes served the maximum term on both the Greater Victoria Public Library Board and the Saanich Police Board and is a past president of the BC Association of Police Boards. He is currently appointed to the Board of Cemetery Trustees.
