The District of Saanich would like to advise the public of a substance release into the Colquitz River.  A sheen was discovered yesterday around noon by Saanich Public Works staff during a routine inspection. The material was found to be seeping from a roadside bank at Interurban and Columbine Way.  Within an hour provincial authorities were contacted, booms were placed in the river to contain and collect the substance, and samples were taken to a local lab to identify both the source and nature of the substance. 

It is unknown how long the material will continue to seep out of the bank. Crews have put up signs advising pedestrians to keep pets and children away from the river’s edge in the immediate downstream area. BC Hydro is on site investigating to determine if the material is escaping from high voltage cables beneath Interurban Road.

Almost one year ago, a major oil spill occurred on Swan Creek.  It was fortunate that many of the returning Coho Salmon were past the spill site when it occurred, however fish mortalities did occur at the fish-counting fence downstream on the Colquitz River. While this current seepage appears to be a gradual release, the District of Saanich notified the volunteers running the fish-counting fence as soon as the spill was known so that they could monitor the situation.

Residents and community environmental stewards are asked to continue to report spills immediately for the health of our streams.
