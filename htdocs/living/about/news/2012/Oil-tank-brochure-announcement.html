New Saanich Heating Oil Tank Brochure

More Information

Heating Oil Tank Brochure

The municipality has developed a new informative Home Heating Oil Tanks brochure for residents who use heating oil systems to heat their homes. Following a number of home heating oil spills in Saanich, the municipality has focused on a proactive community based approach this fall to reduce spills to the natural environment.

The brochure includes tank inspection and preventative maintenance tips, spill response instructions, and emergency contact numbers that all tank owners should have readily accessible. Identifying tank leaks early is the key to preventing spills to the environment and helping homeowners avoid expensive clean-up costs.

“The protection of our waterways and natural habitat is important to all of our residents. I hope that everyone with an oil tank will use this brochure to take a close look at their heating systems to ensure they won’t have to deal with a leaking tank.” says Saanich Councilor Vicki Sanders

The municipality hopes the information in the brochure will help prevent spills like the one that occurred in November 2011 on Swan Creek which resulted in 1,800 litres of heating oil being released to the waterway from a private residence. The spill caused significant contamination of fish habitat and an expensive remediation and monitoring program. In other spill events the homeowner has had to bear the substantial remediation costs.

The brochure is a result of a joint effort between the Municipality’s Planning, Engineering and Fire Departments.
