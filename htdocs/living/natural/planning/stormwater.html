Stormwater is precipitation that runs off the land due to roofs, driveways, sidewalks, patios, etc.  Traditionally, it has been collected and transported from private properties in underground pipes which empty into the closest water body.  Watercourses become deluged with water and banks become unstable.  Pollution from driveways, fertilizers, etc impacts aquatic life if transported by the stormwater.  Drainage pipes need to be maintained and sized to handle all of the stormwater.

New stormwater management requirements are being considered by Saanich, at the request of Saanich Council.  Saanich currently has stormwater management requirements in 4 different bylaws.  The intent is to have all the requirements in one place for better customer service.  Saanich Council has also asked for improvements to stormwater management on private land.
