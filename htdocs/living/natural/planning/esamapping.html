The District of Saanich is expanding its Environmentally Significant Areas (ESA) mapping.  The purpose of this initiative is to improve our existing inventories by looking for areas that may have been missed during past mapping initiatives.  These inventories appear in our three atlases which flag ESA’s, some of which are protected in development permit areas. 

Data collection for this initiative will occur in two phases.  In phase 1, the public are invited to send information on sites that could be of interest which may be visited by our consultant for further scientific data collection.  In phase 2, the consultant will collect data on sites that they have researched through various sources of published information.

What are we looking for?

We are hoping to locate sites that could be added to an existing ESA inventory, as listed below.  Here are a few possibilities and links for further information:

  • Garry Oak and associated ecosystems
  • Seasonal or permanent wetlands
  • Seeps and vernal (temporary) pools
  • Arbutus stands, or trembling aspen groves
  • Non-forested ecosystems
  • Wildlife tree concentrations
  • Urban forest
  • Areas with high restoration potential
  • Modified habitat: hedgerows, barns, old fields

Where do we hope to find new ESA’s?

Saanich is particularly hoping to find new sites near parks which would help to buffer and add to natural areas.  Undeveloped right-of-ways are also important because they can inform future land use.  Villages and centres (see Official Community Plan) are important as we expect to see increased density in the future and knowing where significant areas are in advance is useful.  A balance between urban and rural sites, which are not already protected in some way, is also desired. 

How will the information be used?

The information collected will be used to guide our biological consultant to areas that may be useful additions to our existing inventories and mapping.  The consultant will consider our priorities, the condition of the site, and the scientific merit of including the site as a potential ESA.  Recommendations will be considered to determine if the site is an ESA and if it should appear in one of our atlases.

The deadline for public submissions (Phase 1) has passed. The District of Saanich appreciates all submissions, however not all sites will be visited or considered for ESA potential for a variety of reasons.  Sites will be weighed against set criteria and priorities.  Some sites will be assessed by aerial photo interpretation.  Some private land sites may not be accessible.  For a summary of the methods and results of Phase 1, please see the handout from the public presentation of July 10th.

What if my property is suggested as an ESA?

Both public and private property may be suggested as an ESA.  If suggested, an ESA on your property will be weighed against scientific criteria and priorities.  Our consultant may use aerial photographs to determine if the area is an ESA.  If the consultant wishes to enter your property to gather more information, you will be contacted.  Unless you give permission to the consultant, they will not proceed with gathering information on-site.  After phase 2 is complete, staff and the consultant will work together to recommend additions to our atlases depending on the results of the data collection.  Any land recommended for protection during future development would need to be adopted by Council which includes a Public Hearing.

Our Environmental Atlases

Saanich’s ESA Atlas is used to flag significant areas for information purposes, and contains several inventories, such as:

  • Sensitive Ecosystem Inventory
  • Conservation Data Centre - rare ecosystems, plants and animals
  • Wildlife Trees
  • Saanich Marine Inventory
  • Migratory Bird Sanctuaries
  • Ecological Reserves
  • Natural State Covenants
  • Significant Trees
  • Aquifers
  • Watershed boundaries

The Streamside Development Permit Area (SDPA) Atlas has mapped streams and riparian areas and identifies setbacks to protect streams.  The Environmental Development Permit Area (EDPA) Atlas is used to identify terrestrial and marine environments that are protected during development.
