Saanich Noxious Weeds Bylaw

The District of Saanich has a Noxious Weeds Bylaw, first drafted at the turn of the century to address weeds of threat to agriculture. It has more recently been updated to include other priority plants, including those posing risks to human health.

Through the Noxious Weed Bylaw, property owners in the Municipality of Saanich are required to control noxious weeds (listed on the bylaw—see  below) or any other plants that are “likely to spread or become a nuisance to other…property in the vicinity…”. This description allows invasive species to be included under the bylaw.   Saanich Environmental Services and Bylaw Enforcement work with property owners to assist them in addressing issues.

Local governments are enabled to regulate in areas such as public health, nuisance and protecting the environment.  Noxious weeds and invasive plants can impact all of these areas as well as economic, animal health and other social impacts.  Local governments are also enabled to enforce the Provincial Weed Control Act.

Landowners FAQs:

Q: What do I do if I have a noxious weed on my property?
A: District of Saanich staff from Bylaw Enforcement and Environmental Services are available to advise landowners as to the best practices to remove and control specific plants.  We can provide other information, resources and help you with a plan that will hopefully reduce the time it will take to remove the plant.  If you are unable to conduct the work yourself, you can also hire a professional.

Q: Why am I receiving a formal registered letter from bylaw enforcement because of a weed issue?
A:  The majority of landowners are willing to comply and remove the plant of concern, but occasionally a landowner is unwilling or unavailable to do so.  By sending a registered letter out each year to properties with species of high concern, the District of Saanich is then in a position to take action to have the plant removed.  Most people who receive these letters work cooperatively with the District of Saanich on these issues.

 

Plants currently on Saanich’s Noxious Weed Bylaw:

  • Wild Mustard (Brassica spp.)
  • Canada Thistle (Cirsium arvense)
  • Ox-Eye Daisy (Leucanthemum vulgare)
  • Bindweed Or Morning Glory (Convolvulus & Calystegia spp.)
  • Wild Carrot (Daucus carota)
  • Couchgrass/Quackgrass (Elymus repens)
  • Purple Loosestrife (Lythrum salicaria) (Alert Sheet)
  • Giant Hogweed (Heracleum mantegazzianum) (Alert Sheet)
  • Poison Hemlock (Conium maculatum) (Alert Sheet)
  • Garlic Mustard (Alliaria petiolata) (Alert Sheet)
  • Blessed Milk Thistle (Silybum marianum) (Alert Sheet)
  • Knotweeds (Fallopia spp. & Polygonum spp.) (Alert Sheet)
