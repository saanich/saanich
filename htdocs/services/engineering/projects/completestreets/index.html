What is a Complete Street?


Project Extents

"Complete Streets" are streets that are safe, attractive and provide comfortable access for the young and old, those with mobility impairments, local residents, motorists, pedestrians and cyclists. Complete streets also have the ability to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and help clean stormwater before it enters into biologically sensitive water courses.

Project Background

The District of Saanich identified 3 roads that are in need of upgrading and could benefit from a complete streets design: Cook Street (from Cloverdale Avenue to Maplewood Road), Tillicum Road (from Carey Road to the Trans Canada Highway) and Glanford Avenue (from McKenzie Avenue to Vanalman Avenue).

Through a series of public engagement activities such as sounding board implementation, open houses and surveys the District of Saanich compiled a list of needs and values for each road. Concept Designs for each road were then created following this process.

These concepts will be used as a template during the detail design phase of the projects. Cook Street is currently within the Detailed Design stage and construction is expected in 2014. Tillicum Road and Glanford Avenue detail designs are expected to begin in late 2014.

Details such as concept drawings, milestones and additional material relevant to each street will be included in the street-specific web pages.
