Saanich has over 8,300 street lights of various types and wattages. Nearly every light in Saanich has a photo control switch that tells it to turn on at night and go off during the day. When street lights fail to light up, we rely on our staff and the public to advise us.

Here are a few things to look for:

  • Light is out; this means the lamp, photo control or the fixture itself has failed.
  • Light is going on and off; this is called cycling. this occurs when the lamp is at the end of its service rating or life.
  • Light is staying on during the day; this means the photo control has failed. When they stop working correctly, they fail in the on position.

In all of these cases, we rely on the public to advise us that the light has failed to operate correctly. You can report the problem to our Public Works Department by phoning 250-475-5437, or you can use the form below to report the location and problem.

Additional details may help us.

Is the streetlight in front of, across from, or beside this address given?

If the streetlight is at an intersection, which corner (NW, E, S, etc.), or is it actually on one of the two intersecting streets, or perhaps the only one at the intersection?

If you are aware of a recent problem or repair of the same streetlight please tell us.

If you would like to request that a streetlight be installed on your street, please refer to the section on Local Improvements.
