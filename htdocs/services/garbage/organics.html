How to Participate

If you have food leftovers, soiled paper products or yard trimmings, they can all be placed in your organics recycling cart for collection every second week. About two months later your organics will be converted into compost and used by local farmers.

In the Kitchen

  • Collect your kitchen scraps and food-soiled paper in the kitchen container provided.
  • Use compostable bags to line your kitchen container which will help mitigate odours and leakage and keep your indoor container and outdoor cart clean. If you prefer, use newspaper, paper bags or paper towels to line your container. 
  • See how you can make your own kitchen container liner from newspaper or watch the video !
  • Transfer your kitchen scraps regularly to your outdoor organics recycling cart. 
  • Wash your kitchen container periodically with a mild, non toxic detergent.

In the Yard

  • Add yard trimmings (grass clippings, non-invasive prunings, plants and branches) to your organics recycling cart, making sure they fit inside and the lid closes completely.
  • If you wish to line your cart, use large compostable bags or paper bag liners which are available at grocery stores, drug stores and home hardware stores.
  • If you have more yard trimmings than will fit in the cart, you can save them for your next collection day or bring them to Saanich's Garden Recycling Drop-off at the Public Works Yard.
  • Store your cart in a secure, cool, ventilated location.

At the Curb

  • Put your cart out for collection every second week even if it is not full.
  • On collection day, place your cart at the curb or in the mouth of your driveway, oriented so the lid opens toward the street, and ensure they do not impede sidewalks, traffic or bicycle lanes.

Helpful Tips

  • Empty your kitchen container frequently; wash or rinse containers as needed.
  • Don’t leave kitchen scraps exposed; keep container and cart lids tightly closed.
  • Line the bottom of your container or carts with newspaper or paper towels to absorb moisture or use compostable or paper bags.
  • Freeze meat, fish, poultry or bones and put them in your organics recycling cart on your collection day.
  • Sprinkle a small amount of baking soda, garden lime or vinegar in your containers.
  • Store your carts in a secure, cool, ventilated location.
  • Put your green cart out for collection every second week even if it is not full.

Organics recycling includes:

  • Food leftovers: including meat, fish, poultry, bones, dairy products, bread, solidified fats and grease, fruit and vegetables and plate scrapings.
  • Food-soiled paper products: including paper towels and napkins, food-soiled packaging such as used paper cups and paper plates.
  • Yard trimmings: including grass clippings, non-invasive prunings, plants and branches.

What goes in my organics recycling cart?

Please remember, do NOT place any plastics in your organics recycling cart. They can’t be composted.
