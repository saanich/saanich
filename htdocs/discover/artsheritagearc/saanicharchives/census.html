1911 & 1921 Census Project

Saanich Archives is coordinating a project to transcribe the 1911 and 1921 census data for Saanich. This project will contribute to our understanding of the municipality in the first two decades of the 20th century and will help support our Saanich Remembers World War One project by allowing us to build a picture of Saanich families and neighbourhoods before and after the war. The work will be carried out by community volunteers familiar with our local streets and family names.

Part of the 1911 census has already been transcribed by Automated Genealogy and our project aims to complete the 1911 census and at the same time transcribe the 1921 census for Saanich. Once the project is complete the data will be uploaded to a searchable database at viHistory.ca and will be freely available to the public.

Volunteer Opportunity

Saanich Archives is looking for volunteers to help transcribe the 1911 and 1921 census sheets for Saanich. Volunteers will be asked to read the handwritten census forms and enter the data into a spreadsheet. This work can be done at home on your own computer and the time commitment is flexible.  Some experience with MS Excel is necessary.  Volunteering with this project is a wonderful way to connect to your neighbourhood and build on the strong sense of community that already exists in Saanich.

Historical Background

The 1911 census of Canada began on June 1, 1911. Saanich was part of Census District 10 (Nanaimo). At that time the municipality was divided into administrative areas known as wards which were part of the following enumeration districts:
