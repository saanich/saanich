The data supplied online is extracted from Saanich Archives Visual Records database.

Search results show core information about each photograph - thumbnail, number, title, date.

Click on the thumbnail image to display a larger image. From the larger image you can return to the Search Results thumbnails, scroll using the arrows, or print the page.

Keyword Searching

  • Keyword searches are not case sensitive
  • Do not use punctuation or symbols in the Keyword field
  • For broader results use a truncated word (example: use farm not farms; strawberr instead of strawberry or strawberries)
  • Short phrases may be used
  • Note that search results for common words will produce a high volume of results
  • Keyword searches can be combined with Subject and/or Local Area, example: you can search for photographs of children on a beach in Gordon Head by entering
    • Keyword: beach
    • Subject: Persons-Children
    • Local Area: Gordon Head

Subject Searching

  • Subject terms have been extracted from top level terms in the BC Thesaurus that relate to Saanich (a pdf copy of the full BC Thesaurus is available at http://aabc.bc.ca/aabc/bcthesaurus.pdf)
  • Subject searches can be combined with Keyword and/or Local Area (example: you can search for photographs of schools in different local areas of Saanich)
    • Keyword:
    • Subject: Schools
    • Local Area: Quadra

Local Area Searching

  • Saanich has 12 local areas (Blenkinsop, Cadboro Bay, Carey, Cordova Bay, Gordon Head, North Quadra, Quadra, Royal Oak, Rural Saanich, Saanich Core, Shelbourne, and Tillicum)
  • Two additional terms are included in the Local Area drop down menu: Central Saanich because it was part of the Municipality of Saanich from 1906 to 1950; and Saanich Peninsula, used when a more specific area is not known (example: unidentified pioneer farms on the peninsula)
  • To search all photographs of a Local Area, select the area of interest but leave the Keyword and Subject Field blank (note: for some Local Areas this will produce a large volume of results)
  • To narrow your search results, use the Local Area field in combination with Keyword and/or Subject
