An attractive and appropriate colour scheme is the "finishing touch" for any heritage house. For this reason the Saanich Heritage Foundation (SHF) encourages owners to choose the colours carefully and in some cases to enlist the help of a colour consultant or architect.

An appropriate colour scheme may be one of the following:

  • The original colours as determined by sampling and/or research.
  • A colour scheme from the guidelines found in several reference books which are listed below. Be sure you understand what style your house is  (e.g.) Queen Anne, Craftsman, etc., when referring to these books.

Please note that for a paint job to be funded by the SHF, approval of the colour scheme is required. Payment of the grant is contingent upon the owner following the approved colour scheme. Any alterations to the initially approved colour scheme must also be approved by the SHF.

To Apply for Colour Scheme Approval:

  • Once the owner or a consultant has chosen a colour scheme, it must be illustrated in a clear and concise manner for submission to the SHF. Send completed COLOUR SCHEDULE.
  • Provide colour photographs before and after painting. See PHOTOGRAPH GUIDELINES.
