Fall 2012

Chair's Message

Looking back over the past quarter, CREST continued to make service improvements, some of which had a positive impact on the use of the system during Canada Day - our single largest annual event.

We've had the pleasure of presenting to a number of our shareholders over the past few months - Esquimalt, Oak Bay and Victoria with a couple more scheduled this fall with Metchosin and North Saanich. These are an opportunity to discuss the benefits of CREST, the relationship that exists between shareholders, users and ourselves, and to share information on work being done to continually maintain and upgade the system.

These and other steps are helping to reach our goal of building user and public confidence in the system and the company. Please contact us if you would like to learn more. 

Taxpayers Taking Double Hit on 911

A BC Supreme Court decision made in July quashed an attempt to obtain the funds that telephone companies collect for 911 services.

In response, CREST issued a news release saying property owners are being double billed for 911 service. Chair Gordie Logan said, "we are paying for this service through our property taxes and again through our phone provider." 

CREST has asked Justice Minister Shirley Bond to bring in legislation to stop the double billing. 

Improvements Continue

Recent system improvements include a new transmit site on the West Shore that went live last month. A number of steps have been, and are, being taken to improve reception and reliability in the Shirley area, and a site has been identified for a new receiver in Esquimalt.

Equipment added to VicPD's dispatch for Canada Day resulted in improved voice quality and operations. VicPD Director Darlene Perry said in an email that the base stations worked very well in the EOC (Emergency Operations Centre) and that dispatchers found it much better.

New Web Site Launched

Check out the new and improved CREST web site for up-to-date information on system performance, use stats, what's new and more. 

The new site includes a secure area for users to access on-line training programs for CREST radios and discussion forums. 

CREST Showcased at UVic Job Fair

To ensure that CREST continues to attract the best and the brightest in radio communications, staff participated in a Hi-tech Co-op and Career Fair at UVic this spring. Here, students learn about the radio technology and networking in use at CREST. 

CHEK Around Features CREST

To help increase understanding of the benefits of the CREST system, CHEK Around host Gordie Tupper interviewed chair Gordie Logan and users from the Saanich Police and Fire departments for this short clip. It ran on CHEK-TV in late July and early August.
