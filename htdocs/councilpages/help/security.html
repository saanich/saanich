iPad Secure Use Gudelines - Paperless Council Project

The most effective security measure for the mobile devices is to follow the best preventive practices, and requires a firm commitment from all users of mobile technology.

All users should follow appropriate guidelines for setting passwords on mobile devices as well as enable the device to be remotely wiped of all data and locked to prevent access by anyone other than IT:

Protect

  • Do not leave devices unattended in vulnerable locations such as offices, airports, and hotels
  • Keep out of sight when not in use in a public place (which may include your work station)
  • Prevent physical damage by using standard handling procedures available on the manufacturer’s website
  • Recovery costs may be incurred by  your department ? user? if loss or damage is due to negligence on the part of the user

Secure

Set Up a Passcode:

To set up or change a passcode:
Settings > General > Passcode lock > enter a passcode

More instructions: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iocKklPgVUc

 Encrypt Data:

No need to worry about security of your data while in transfer through the Network. iPad offers encoding hardware-based encryption to protect all data on the device. Encryption is always enabled and cannot be disabled by users. iPad provides encrypted protection of data in transit, at rest, or backed up to iTunes.

Locate and Erase:

If you lose your iPad, Find My iPad can help you locate it on a map, display a message, or remotely delete your data. How?

  • Sign in to iCloud.com from any web browser on a Mac or PC. Or borrow your friend’s iPad, iPhone, or iPod touch and use the FindMyiPad app. FindMyiPad shows you the approximate location of your iPad on a map.
  • Display a message or play a sound to help you find it.
  • If someone finds your iPad, let them know how to find you. Write a message and display it on your iPad screen. Something like, “Oops, left my iPad behind. Please call me at 408-555-0198.” Your message appears, even if the screen is locked. And if the map shows that your iPad is nearby — perhaps in your office under a pile of papers — yet you still can’t find it, you can tell FindMyiPad to play a sound that overrides the volume or silent setting.
  • if you lose your iPad and displaying a message on it hasn’t resulted in its safe return, you can initiate a remote wipe to restore it to its factory settings http://www.apple.com/ipad/built-in-apps/find-my-ipad.html
  • By default, iPad will automatically wipe the device after 10 failed passcode attempts.

Note: It will work only if FindMyiPad has been previously installed and enabled. How? Settings > tap iCloud > enable FindMyiPad.

For more information see iPad – Q&A on this website.
