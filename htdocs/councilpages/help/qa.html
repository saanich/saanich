5. Why did I receive SIM card info with my iPad?

If you received a SIM card, it means that you have 3G iPad model with the micro-SIM card that is used for cellular data download. Your micro-SIM card was already pre-installed and you are on data plan, similar to the data plan when you operate a Smartphone. There is a monthly fee for this data plan that Saanich covers.
Your iPad will automatically switch to a Wi-Fi network if a Wi-Fi is available in the area.

See page 29 of the manual: http://manuals.info.apple.com/en_US/ipad_2_user_guide.pdf

6. What is the difference between WiFi and 3G iPad models?

The 3G enabled iPad will work in the field without the need to be connected to the WiFi network. It requires a SIM card and a data plan, similar to data plan when you operate a Smartphone.

The Wi-Fi model will only operate in locations where free Wi-Fi signals are found.

7. I have a wireless connection at home, how do I switch between the data plan and Wi-Fi?

iPad will switch automatically from data plan to wireless if Wi-Fi is on. Make sure that Wi -Fi is enabled on your iPad. Choose Settings > Wi-Fi and turn Wi-Fi on. 

The iPad model you use is listed as "Wi-Fi + 3G". It offers both: 1) the standard Wi-Fi connectivity 2) plus the option to sign up for 3G cellular service with specific networks (Telus for example) and get a connection to the Internet when Wi-Fi is unavailable.

  1. The standard Wi-Fi connectivity
    (iPad can join Wi-Fi networks at home, at work, or at Wi-Fi hotspots around the world and there is no cost and no limit to amount of data you can download).
    Make sure that Wi -Fi is enabled on your iPad. Choose Settings > Wi-Fi and turn Wi-Fi on.

  2. We already signed up your iPad for 3G cellular service /data plan. Your micro-SIM card was already pre-installed and you are on data plan, similar to the data plan when you operate a Smartphone. There is a monthly fee for this data plan that Saanich covers. The data plan for all of our iPads is set at $20 per month for up to 500 MB. Overage is charged at $0.05 per MB.
    For most our iPad users this plan is sufficient enough. For other users it may not be the case, depending on how much data you need to download every day.

    > Your iPad will automatically switch to Wi-Fi network if Wi-Fi is available in the area. See page 29 of the user manual: http://manuals.info.apple.com/en_US/ipad_2_user_guide.pdf
    > If you decide you need to be able to download more data, you can upgrade your data plan.

    Read more: http://www.ehow.com/info_8258431_3g-mean-ipad.html#ixzz1nz9V5rdC

    Roaming is another rate as well. See “Roaming: what you need to know before you roam”.

10. How do I setup my email account?

1)  From the main screen select ‘Settings’

2) Scroll down to ‘Mail, Contacts, Calendars’

3) Select your e-mail provider - do you use free email service (for example Gmail, Yahoo, Hotmail) or have a paid account with the carrier such as Telus, Rogers, Shaw?

>> If you use a free email service, tap the appropriate provider:

>> If you have a paid account with the email provider such as Telus, Shaw, Saanich.ca etc. then tap ‘Other’

5)  Select ‘Add Mail Account’

6)  Fill out the ‘Name’, ‘Address’, ‘Password’, ‘Description’ fields, and select ‘Next’
 

7)  Ensure that ‘IMAP’ is selected and fill out the ‘Host Name’, ‘Username’, and ‘Password field’
 

* The hostname will vary depending on the organizations email settings (for example, if your phone provider is Telus, host name will be: imap.telus.net)
Contact your email provider for Host Name, User Name and Password
A list of other possible carriers and their respective SMTP address:
Saanich E-mail Hostname: gw.saanich.ca
Shaw E-mail Hostname: pop.shaw.ca
Rogers:  smtp.rogerswirelessdata.com)
Koodo: smtp.telus.net
Fido: gprs.fido.ca
Bell:  mail.1xbell.ca

8)  Scroll down to ‘Outgoing Mail Server’ fields and fill out ‘Hostname’

* Just like above, the hostname will vary depending on the service provider.

9)  Select ‘Next’
An error stating ‘Cannot Connect Using SSL’ this is expected, select ‘Yes’

10)  The same prompt will come up again, select ‘Yes’

11) Ensure ‘Mail’ in switched to the ‘On’ position and press ‘Save’

12) You should now be receiving your E-mail on your iPad device

16. How to use FaceTime.

FaceTime is easy to set up. All you need is an Apple ID and an email address, and you’re ready to go. To start a FaceTime call, tap your contact’s phone number if she/he has an iPhone 4, or tap her/his email address to call an iPad 2, iPod touch, or Mac. An invitation pops up on her screen, and when she accepts, the call begins. See the video: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWQNYwTafZo&feature=relmfu

You can switch between the two built-in cameras on iPad 2 anytime during a call. The front camera faces you, and the back camera faces what’s in front of you, so you can show where you are or share visual details from a job site.

17. How to locate a misplaced iPad.

If you lose your iPad, Find My iPad can help you locate it on a map, display a message, or remotely delete your data.
(Note: It will work only if iCloud has been previously installed on your iPad and FindMyiPad has been enabled. How? See the next question for instructions).

  • Sign in using your Apple ID to iCloud.com from any web browser on a Mac or PC or borrow your friend’s iPad, iPhone, or iPod touch and use the FindMyiPad app previously installed. How? Go to http://www.apple.com/icloud/setup/ > scroll to the bottom of the page > click Get the Find My iPhone app
  •  FindMyiPad shows you the approximate location of your iPad on a map.
  • Display a message or play a sound to help you find it.
  • If someone finds your iPad, let them know how to find you. Write a message and display it on your iPad screen. Something like, “Oops, left my iPad behind. Please call me at 408-555-0198.” Your message appears, even if the screen is locked. And if the map shows that your iPad is nearby — perhaps in your office under a pile of papers — yet you still can’t find it, you can tell FindMyiPad to play a sound that overrides the volume or silent setting.
  • If displaying a message on it hasn’t resulted in its safe return, you can initiate a remote wipe.

20. How to streamline presentation from an iPad to a large screen or TV .

AirPlay Mirroring lets you wirelessly stream whatever’s on your iPad screen to your HDTV via Apple TV.  Setting up Apple TV is about as simple as it gets. Just plug the power cord into the wall and connect Apple TV to your widescreen TV using an HDMI cable (sold separately).

1) Go to the AirPlay setting on the Apple TV and make sure it's turned on
2)  Make sure your TV is on and set to the correct input that your Apple TV is connected to
3) When you display a video or presentation on your iPad, there's an icon on the iPad's playbar that looks like a TV. Just tap it and select Apple TV and it will send it to the TV
4) If you want to mirror everything that you do on the iPad to the TV, double tap the Home button, swipe right, and you'll see the same icon there. Click on it, tap on Apple TV, then turn mirroring on.

21. How to print a document from an iPad.

Printing is available only with AirPrint-enabled printers. iPad automatically locates and connects to AirPrint-enabled printers on your Wi-Fi network.
1 Tap on the screen, then tap Print.
2 Tap Select Printer to select a printer.
3 Set printer options, such as number of copies and double-sided output (if the printer supports it). Some apps also let you set a range of pages to print.
4 Tap Print

Currently AirPrint-enabled printers are not available at Saanich. SaanichIT is working towards enabling this functionality in the future, as a part of the strategy to offer a broader range of end user devices, and enabling staff  to choose the type of device that fits best their work  requirements. See Next Generation Desktop project information on the SaanichIT page on eLink.
You can email the document to yourself and print it on your printer in the office or at home.
