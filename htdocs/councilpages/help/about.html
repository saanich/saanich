Emerging Technology Program @ Saanich

With mainstream availability of devices, such as smartphones, tablets, laptops and business applications for them, many organizations have been successful in using mobile and personal devices to dramatically reduce their use of paper and increase productivity. From any location, these devices provide access to documents, easier reading of documents, convenient access to applications such as email, and highly efficient browsing access to the internet.

The Emerging Technology Program at Saanich is one component of a wider enterprise IT initiative to provide the organization with ability to offer a broader range of end user devices, and enabling staff to choose the type of device that fits best their work requirements. It supports the following corporate objectives:

  • Increasing productivity within business units
  • Increasing citizen, customer and staff satisfaction
  • Environmental sustainability (paperless job activities)

Emerging Technology Program is piloting the delivery of new technology into areas of the organization where it can lead to productivity gains beyond the pure cost of the devices.

Projects

By 2013, it is expected that every worker will be bringing a commercial device into the workplace.

Risks and costs of doing nothing about personal and unmanaged devices are greater than the risks and costs of investing the time and resources to develop a strategy, define processes, and apply technical controls. Consumerization is here, and organizations need to respond accordingly.

Placement of new devices in the hands of our employees assists SaanichIT to:

  • manage the explosion in mobile devices on the corporate network, while maintaining security
  • design adequate support processes to deliver this technology in a sustainable way
  • anticipate future technology requirements and expectations in the organization
  • validate that the deeper investments in infrastructure will support the future technology needs of Saanich

Possible Benefits

Time and cost savings:

  • Higher productivity due to remote emailing, calendaring, and video conferencing
  • Higher productivity when distributing contracts, policies, briefings, and other large documents
  • Decrease in time and cost due to remote troubleshooting
  • Higher efficiency when troubleshooting and using video chat for "hands on" experience
  • Replacement for pricey laptop solutions (when applicable)
  • Reduction in time and effort by enabling electronic distribution of correspondence and documents

A significant reduction of paper usage:

  • Electronic distribution of large documents: agendas, briefings, meeting minutes
  • Taking notes electronically, document mark-up, and sharing

Just-in-time learning:

  • Increased learning efficiency and lower cost of training due to ability to access learning information anytime, from anywhere
  • Increased learning efficiency and lower cost of training due to Just-in-time training (access to chat, video chat, training videos, manuals, etc. right on the job, and immediately, when needed)
